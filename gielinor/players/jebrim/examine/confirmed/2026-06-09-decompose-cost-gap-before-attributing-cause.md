# Decompose a cost gap before attributing its cause — verify the comparison framing from source

**2026-06-09 ([[S175_6c5170d1_routing-cost-basis-review|S175]], sid 6c5170d1).** Across the DPD/DHL cost-basis review I repeatedly named the *cause* or *framing* of a cost delta before grounding it, and was wrong each time until I ran the decomposition / read the source:

- Claimed DHL's 2026 rates were "already in Q1 actuals" — wrong; effective **2026-03-01** (Niklavs corrected).
- Had the DPD current-vs-new offer **polarity backwards** (the `1. EU` folder file is the CURRENT contract; Niklavs corrected).
- Hypothesized the DPD +19% driver **twice** (CH customs; then service-mismatch) — the component decomposition showed it was **base-rate + the €0.20 flat-services stack**.
- Said NL/BE "kept the cheap flow at comparable rates" — too loose; they're **+12-14%** (Niklavs: *"be very clear"*).

**Lesson.** For a cost delta: (1) decompose to component/driver from the data, and (2) verify the comparison basis — which tier / service / flow / rate-year, and which file is current vs new — from the **source documents**, BEFORE characterizing the cause. Don't narrate the likely driver from intuition; the decomposition is cheap and the intuition was wrong repeatedly here. Sibling of the contract-tier-vs-actuals and read-the-rate-type-column lessons.
