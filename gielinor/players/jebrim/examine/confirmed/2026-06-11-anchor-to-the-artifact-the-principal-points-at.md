# Anchor to the artifact the principal is pointing at — don't let a keyword redirect the view

**Type:** examine draft (self-observation, Q5 correction harvest) · **Session:** [[S217_4e69b79c_eu-tender-final-report-savings-decomposition|S217]] / 4e69b79c · **Date:** 2026-06-11

**The moment.** Mid-iteration on the final report, Niklavs said *"size the saving per service so we know better which need to be verified,"* referring to "the view we created before." I latched onto the keyword **"service"** and started toward the **service-mix table** (the only view with a service column) — read both build scripts to plan adding saving-per-service. He corrected: *"ooh wait, i see the misunderstanding. I wanted 2 more columns here"* with a **screenshot of the from→to matrix** — he wanted cost-now / cost-after columns on the matrix we'd just built, not a new per-service cut.

**The miss.** "The view we created before" + a screenshot pinned the artifact unambiguously — the matrix. I let a single word ("service") pull me to a *different* view before confirming against the thing he was actually looking at. The grounding cue had even fired ("the view we created before"), and I named my referent reading — but I reasoned about the referent from the keyword instead of from the pointed-at artifact.

**Why it was cheap this time (and the lesson holds anyway).** I'd only *read files* to plan, not built the wrong thing — and I surfaced my reading, so he caught it in one line. But the default should be: when the principal references "this/the view" — especially with a screenshot or against the most-recent artifact — **anchor to that artifact and confirm the exact change (which columns/rows) before generalizing from any keyword in the sentence.** A screenshot is the strongest possible referent; read it as the anchor, not as backdrop.

**Rule.** "Do X to the view we built" + an image/recent artifact → the artifact is the referent. Confirm the concrete edit against it; don't let a noun in the ask reroute you to a sibling view. Sibling of [[anchor_referent_before_analyzing]] and [[match_artifact_family_by_reading_it]] (read the actual artifact, don't infer) — this is the *which-artifact* variant: a keyword tried to swap the artifact out from under a clear pointer.
