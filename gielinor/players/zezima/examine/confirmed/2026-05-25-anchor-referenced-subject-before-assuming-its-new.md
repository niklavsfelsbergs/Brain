# Anchor a referenced subject to prior work before assuming it's new

**Observation.** In [[S066_e2362ea0_domajamas-dalas-reconsider|S066]] the principal opened with *"I want to talk about this property some more.
Im considering buying it afterall"* and a link I hadn't seen. The listing's address and details
(Ģertrūdes 113, pre-war, 58 m²) didn't match the [[S056_e5df54a2_apartment-domajamas-dalas-research|S056]] records as I'd skimmed them, so I concluded it
was a **new, third property** and wrote a whole substantive response on that frame ("this isn't the
flat we walked from — don't apply the old WALK"). The principal corrected: *"nono this is the one, I
just didnt share the link before."* It was [[S056_e5df54a2_apartment-domajamas-dalas-research|S056]] apartment #1.

**The miss.** The grounding reads were good (I pulled the [[S056_e5df54a2_apartment-domajamas-dalas-research|S056]] quest-log, bank notes, research). The
error was an **identity inference**: continuation language — *"this property… some more,"
"afterall"* — signals the subject is **already in the brain**, yet I let mismatching surface details
override that signal and assumed novelty.

**Rule.** When the principal refers to something with continuation cues ("this X again," "some
more," "afterall," "back to X"), treat the subject as **presumptively known** — map it to existing
quests/research first. If surface details don't match what's on file, **ask "is this the same one?"**
before building an analysis on the novelty assumption. A wrong identity inference wastes a full
response. (Sibling to [[grounding-before-advice]]: grounding is necessary but not sufficient — the
referent has to be *identified*, not just researched.)
