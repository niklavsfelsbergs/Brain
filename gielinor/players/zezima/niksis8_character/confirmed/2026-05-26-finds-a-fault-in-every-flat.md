# Niklavs finds a fault in every flat — the gut "no" isn't a detector, it's a reflex

**Observation (2026-05-26, Ģertrūdes 113 deliberation).** Niklavs, verbatim:
*"Problem is that I find something wrong with any flat... but if I keep going like this I will
never buy anything."* And of the *domājamās daļas* structure he had just had fully de-risked:
*"yeah, it just sounds dirty tbh."* Earlier in the same thread he'd noted the flat isn't in his
favourite area — *"but that's also why it's cheaper."*

**The pattern.** His default stance toward a home-buy is fault-finding, and the search always
succeeds — there is no flawless flat, so "I found something wrong" carries almost no signal; it was
always going to be true. The faults he lands on tend to be **flaws-with-a-compensating-discount**
(Avoti location → cheaper; share structure → ~30% off): how value works, not defects. Part of the
resistance is **visceral/aesthetic** ("sounds dirty"), not analytical, and survives even after the
analysis clears the substance. He recognises the pattern himself and that it risks never buying.

**How to apply (Zezima).**
- His gut **"no" fires on everything**, so it can't be trusted as a veto on any specific flat. Don't
  help him hunt for the disqualifying flaw — it's always findable, and finding it resolves nothing.
- Reframe the choice as **"this flat vs. continuing to pay to search for a flawless one that doesn't
  exist."** His rent (~€6k/yr) is the *ongoing cost of the pattern* — name it; it's the quiet,
  compounding risk he discounts while fixating on the vivid, nameable one.
- Shift the decision rule from **"buy when nothing's wrong"** (never happens) to **"buy when the good
  outweighs the known, priced-in imperfection."**
- Distinguish a flaw that's a genuine dealbreaker *for his life* (would make him miserable living
  there) from one that's merely **unfamiliar** ("sounds dirty"). Honour the first; discount the second.
- **Don't overcorrect into pushing a buy to break the paralysis** — over-caution isn't cured by
  recklessness. The job is to make the real trade visible, not to talk him into anything.
