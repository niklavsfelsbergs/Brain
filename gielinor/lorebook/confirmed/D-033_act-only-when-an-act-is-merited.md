# Act only when an act is clearly merited — a question is for thinking, not dispatching

**Status:** confirmed as D-033 ([[B-017_2026-06-03_seventeenth-bankstanding|B-017]], 2026-06-03).

## The rule

When the principal asks a question, the default response is to **answer and discuss** — not to execute. A question is the principal working the problem *with* me, figuring out the approach. It is not an instruction to go build, write files, or take action.

The bar for "act now" is an **explicit, clear indication that an act is wanted** — "do it", "go ahead", "write it up", a direct imperative. A question I *could* answer by doing something is not that. Neither is my own judgment that an action would be helpful.

When unsure whether a turn is "discuss" or "do," default to discuss and surface the action as an offer the principal can take or decline — but do not pre-execute the offer.

## Observation (anchor)

2026-06-02, transit-times session. The principal asked *"how to ensure that you read carefully instead of locking on 1 word?"* — a question to settle the *approach* to a fix. I treated it as a build order and immediately wrote a skill file, then offered to write more. Repeated the pattern one turn later. The principal: *"When I ask a question — DON'T START DOING, I'm clearly trying to figure out the approach we will take instead of letting you lead."*

The failure: I converted exploratory questions into execution. It cost trust and forced a correction, and it pre-committed structure (a written file) before the approach was even agreed — the opposite of the cheap-correction logic the Understanding/Plan preamble exists for.

## How to apply

- Question (how / what / why / "should we" / "what do you think") → answer, discuss, lay out options, recommend. **No file writes, no commits, no irreversible moves** unless separately and explicitly told to act.
- Explicit imperative ("do it", "build it", "write it") → act.
- Ambiguous → answer + offer; let the principal pull the trigger.
- This is the same family as the Minimal-action lens in `communication-protocol.md` (smallest thing that satisfies the ask) and the anti-over-build memory ([[don't over-formalize a reachable capability]]) — generalized to: when in doubt, the smallest satisfying action to a *question* is a good answer.
