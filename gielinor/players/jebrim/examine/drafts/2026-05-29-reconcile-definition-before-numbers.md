# Reconcile the definition before reconciling the numbers

**Observation ([[S123_4a7041b5_b2b-b2c-strict-definition|S123]], 2026-05-29).** The principal handed over a post-mortem: a prior WICKEL-60×40 routing analysis claimed "B2B → UPS," the principal countered "81.5% of B2B is Maersk," and the prior agent's walk-back fixed the *wrong* error — it owned a confident causal overclaim ("deliberate routing rule") but never noticed the two figures described **different populations**. "B2B" meant the `b2b-shop` *storefront* in one breath and MerchOne/`PicaAPI` (the platform) in the next — its own line "99.9% of B2B→UPS, 100% of MerchOne→Maersk" contradicted itself, since MerchOne *is* B2B.

**The rule.** When two figures carrying the *same label* point in opposite directions, suspect a **definitional equivocation** (the label resolves to different populations) *before* reaching for a causal explanation or re-running the math. A contradiction between same-named numbers is more often a fork in the definition than an error in either number. Reconcile what the label *means* — pin it to a single computable rule against ground truth — then the numbers reconcile themselves. Fixing the cause-story while the definition stays ambiguous entrenches the confusion.

**Diagnostic tell:** if your own number (b2b-shop → 96% UPS) flatly contradicts the principal's (81.5% Maersk), that gap is the signal you're counting different things — not a prompt to defend your number.
