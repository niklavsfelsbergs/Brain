# Window-bounded series can invert incumbency — pull the long history before classifying a transition

**Observation ([[S197_b93204b5_q04e-label-churn-deep-dive|S197]], sid8 b93204b5, 2026-06-11).** Investigating the q04d packagetype relabels from the Q1-only population parquet, I provisionally classified STANZVERPACKUNG 120x90→120x80 as "old label → new label migration" (quest-log T2 interim read: 120x90 = the incumbent being replaced). The shipping-agent's 2024→2026 mart pull inverted it: 120x80 had been the dominant box for two years; the Dec 2025–Jan 2026 swing INTO 120x90 was the anomaly, and the "Jan 28 switch" was a reversion to the long-run state. A Q1 window sees exactly the inverted half. Same session, second instance: GEL's late-March "death" in the parquet (60/wk→0) didn't reproduce in the mart — an invoiced-only/invoice-lag extract artifact, not a real stop.

**The pattern.** A window-bounded extract shows direction-of-change relative to the window's start, not relative to the entity's history. Whichever label/carrier/state dominates early-window reads as "the incumbent" — if the window opens mid-anomaly, the classification flips sign. And the extract's *basis filters* (invoiced-only) manufacture fake deaths at the window edge.

**Rule.** Before classifying any transition (relabel, switch, migration, death) from a windowed extract: (1) pull the entity's longer history from the live source — the window's "old" may be the anomaly; (2) treat window-edge disappearances as extract artifacts until the live source confirms them. I did both here *by planned verification* (the mart pull was in the brief) — the lesson is that the interim window-based read was confidently wrong in direction, so the long-history pull is load-bearing, not confirmatory garnish.
