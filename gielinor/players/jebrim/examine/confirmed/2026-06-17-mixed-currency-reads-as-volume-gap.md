This session I built a LucaNet-vs-BI revenue table with **LucaNet in USD and BI in EUR shown side by side, no conversion**. Niklavs read it as "why do we have so much *less* revenue in BI?" — the ~13% that was just the EUR/USD rate looked like a real volume shortfall. (Image #1, the Summary headline.)

Lesson: in any side-by-side comparison, **convert to ONE currency before showing absolute figures** — never place EUR next to USD in a headline. A unit difference reads as a real difference. Ratios (quota) are FX-neutral and safe to compare directly. Fixed by converting BI→USD with a Gap column and a leading "this is a units artifact" note.

Generalized to cross-conversation memory: `feedback_no_mixed_currency_side_by_side`.
