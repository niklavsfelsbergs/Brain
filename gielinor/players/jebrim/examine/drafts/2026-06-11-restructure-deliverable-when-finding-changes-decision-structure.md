# A finding that changes the decision structure demands a narrative rebuild, not a callout

**Anchor (S194-continuation / sid8 907d4e63, 2026-06-11).** The Hermes-DBS dependency analysis (86% of Hermes' value rides on the gated reroute) first landed in the final report as a warning callout + reordered asks. Niklavs: *"okay but this is like a huge change of narrative for the whole report. It all needs to be reworked a bit with this context."* The fix was structural: the headline itself re-cut from a confidence split (committed €472k) to a decision split (base €420k signable now + a €578k oversize module gated on one check) — the old "committed" number had quietly contained €52k of module-dependent value.

**The rule.** When a finding re-partitions the *options* — creates a dependency that binds two previously-separate decisions, or splits one decision into a now/conditional pair — the deliverable's primary structure must be rebuilt around the new partition. A callout bolted onto the old structure leaves the headline selling a frame the finding just invalidated; readers act on headlines, not callouts.

**The test.** After any material finding, ask: does the report's top-level cut still match the decision the reader actually faces? If the finding moved the decision line, move the structure. Sibling of rebuild-from-concepts (dev brain) — same lesson at document scale.
