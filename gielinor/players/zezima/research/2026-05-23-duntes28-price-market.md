# Duntes iela 28 ("Duntes Ozoli") — price & resale market

**Question.** Is €120,000 / €1,714 per m² fair for the Duntes iela 28 apartment (2 rooms, 70.4 m², 9th of 16 floors, brick, 2007, excellent condition, courtyard parking + 8.6 m² loggia), and what's the resale/liquidity outlook?

**Date of research.** 2026-05-23.

**Confidence.** Medium-high. Price-fairness verdict is well-anchored (multiple independent district + project-level €/m² benchmarks, all current to May 2026). Liquidity and rent figures are anchored but thinner (a couple of live comparables, one aggregate). Live listing-by-listing comps were partly limited by JS-rendered portal pages I couldn't fully extract.

## Findings

### 1. Comparables — €1,714/m² is BELOW market for this segment

The single most decisive number: the May-2026 asking-price benchmarks for the relevant geography all sit **above** €1,714/m²:

| Benchmark (asking, May 2026) | €/m² | Asking vs €1,714 |
|---|---|---|
| Pētersala un Andrejsala | 2,033 | **−16% (cheaper)** |
| Riga overall | 1,730 | −1% (at market) |
| Centrs | 2,478 | −31% |
| Skanste | 2,777 | −38% |
| New projects (Riga) | ~2,549 | −33% |
| Duntes Ozoli — 2-room avg | ~2,000 | **−14%** |
| Duntes Ozoli — building avg | ~1,900 | −10% |
| Pre-2000 / Soviet series | ~1,517 | +13% (dearer) |
| Sarkandaugava district avg | ~1,071 | +60% (much dearer) |

So the answer depends entirely on **which neighborhood frame you apply**, and Duntes 28 sits on a real boundary:

- **As a new(ish)-project / Pētersala-Centrs-adjacent unit** (which is how Duntes Ozoli is marketed and priced — multiple portals tag it "Centrā" and the project's own 2-room average is ~€2,000/m²), **€1,714/m² is meaningfully below market — roughly 10–16% under comparable asking.** A favorable buy.
- **As a generic Sarkandaugava-district unit** (asking avg €1,071/m², closed deals €811/m²), €1,714/m² looks *expensive*. But that district average is dominated by old/Soviet stock and small flats (2-room avg 46 m² @ €48.5k) — not comparable to a 70 m² 2007 secured-project apartment with parking and loggia.

The honest read *(inference from the spread)*: Duntes Ozoli is a quality-tier outlier sitting inside a cheap postal district. The right comparison set is the **project itself + Pētersala/Centrs new-ish stock**, not the Sarkandaugava blanket average. Against that correct frame, **€1,714/m² is a good price — below the project's own 2-room average and well below new-build replacement cost.**

Live in-project comps corroborate: a 103 m² 3-room in the same building asks **€1,500/m²** (rentinriga #52035) — but larger units always carry a lower €/m², so a 70 m² 2-room legitimately commands more per m² than that. €1,714 for 70 m² is internally consistent with the building's €1,500 (103 m²) ↔ €2,000 (56 m² avg) gradient.

### 2. Riga market context 2026 — stable, modestly rising, new-build premium widening

- **Trend: flat-to-modestly-up.** 2025 closed ~+5% YoY; cumulative +30% since 2022 (construction-cost push). 2026 forecast is "stability + moderate growth," not a boom. (City24 expert panel.)
- **Riga is the Baltic laggard.** Ober-Haus indicator shows Riga roughly flat / −0.9% while Tallinn (+4.9%) and Vilnius (+4.8%) ran ahead. Less froth = less downside risk, but also less rapid appreciation.
- **New projects appreciate faster than series stock**, and under-construction units carry a **€100–200/m² premium over completed ones** — meaning a *completed, 2007* building like this won't enjoy the new-build appreciation curve, but its entry price already reflects that (it's priced as established, not new).
- **Demand-side tailwind:** declining Euribor, average mortgage up ~€10k to ~€95k, first-time buyers active. A €120k mortgage-financed owner-occupier purchase sits squarely in the most active demand band.

### 3. Liquidity — moderately liquid; the price point is the sweet spot

- **The €100–160k 2-room band is the most liquid segment of the Riga market.** Buyer pool = first-time owner-occupiers (Euribor-driven, the most active 2026 cohort) and small landlord-investors.
- **In-project turnover:** Duntes Ozoli logged 6–11 recorded transactions/year 2022–2025 (viszinis), so units do trade regularly — not a frozen building. For a single project that's healthy churn.
- **Caveat — Sarkandaugava is a thin district:** only ~3% of all Riga sale offers, and closed deals there ran ~24% below asking. That discount is partly negotiation, partly old-stock drag — but it's a flag that *sellers in this postal area accept meaningful haircuts*. A quality unit should beat the district average on both speed and price-realization, but expect buyers to anchor on the cheap district reputation and negotiate.
- **Net (inference):** sellable within a normal market window (weeks-to-few-months) at the right price; **not** a flip-fast asset, and resale will require educating buyers that it's a quality project, not a generic Sarkandaugava flat. Owner-occupier horizon (which is the plan) neutralizes most of this.

### 4. Rent check — ~€470–550/mo gross, ~4.5–5% gross yield

- Direct in-building anchor: a 57 m² 2-room in Duntes Ozoli rented at **€470/mo (€8.3/m²)** + utilities (rentinriga #3891). Scaling €8.3/m² to 70.4 m² → **~€580/mo**; but larger units rent at slightly lower €/m², so realistic range **€470–550/mo** for the 70 m² unit. *(inference from €/m² scaling + the LSM market note that 2-room flats cluster at €300–500 unfurnished, new projects €550–600.)*
- **Gross yield on €120k:** €500/mo × 12 = €6,000/yr → **~5.0% gross** (≈4.5% if rent is nearer €450). Decent for Riga; typical Riga residential gross yields run 4–6%. Net yield lower after management/vacancy/tax.
- **Rent-vs-buy:** at ~€500 rent vs a €120k purchase, the buy is reasonable given the sub-market entry price; the yield says it would also work as a rental if his plans change — useful optionality even though he intends to occupy.

### 5. Price sanity flags

- **Not suspiciously cheap.** €1,714/m² is below comparable asking but *above* the building's floor (€1,100–1,500/m² showed up only for large 3–6 room units, where low €/m² is normal). It's consistent with the building's own size/price gradient. No "too cheap → hidden defect" signal from the price alone. *(Construction/management-quality risk is P7's scope — not assessed here.)*
- **Not overpriced.** It's 10–16% under the project's 2-room average and the Pētersala benchmark, and ~33% under Riga new-build asking. A 2007 building legitimately trades below 2020s new-builds (which ask €2,500–3,600/m² in this area) — so the gap to new construction is *expected and correctly priced*, not a red flag.
- **The one yellow flag:** the Sarkandaugava district reputation (cheap, thin, deals close ~24% under asking) is a *resale-narrative* risk, not a *current-price* risk. He's buying below market; the watch-item is whether the next buyer applies the project frame or the district frame.
- **2007 vs 2020s new-builds:** newer buildings ask €2,500–3,600/m² locally and appreciate faster, but cost ~50–100% more per m². For an owner-occupier on a budget, the 2007 brick project at €1,714 is the value play — established, quality-tier, secured, with parking, at well under new-build pricing.

---

## Bottom line

**€120,000 / €1,714/m² is a fair-to-favorable price** when correctly framed against the Duntes Ozoli project (~€2,000/m² 2-room avg) and the Pētersala/Centrs-adjacent new-ish segment (€2,033–2,478/m²) — roughly 10–16% under comparable asking. It only looks expensive against the raw Sarkandaugava district average, which is the wrong comp set (old/small stock). Market is stable with modest 2026 upside; the €100–160k 2-room band is the most liquid segment; rent optionality is ~€470–550/mo (~4.5–5% gross yield). Main resale watch-item: the cheap-district reputation means a future buyer may negotiate hard — but he's entering below market, which is the cushion.
