# The "legitimate/explained" bucket needs the same falsifying drill as the disputed ones

**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Session:** ee882f39 (UPS OML/LPS predictor investigation) · **Type:** self-observation (Jebrim)

**Anchor (verbatim):** "425k legitimate - I dont believe we have so much parcels shipped which exceed 325 - show me what they are in that case"

**What happened.** I presented a three-way verdict split and labeled €425k "legitimate — parcels above 325 by our own declared dims," treating that bucket as settled because it matched the trigger rule. Niklavs didn't believe the volume and asked to see the parcels. The drill showed: 94% of the bucket is ONE box (zugeschnittene Verpackung, 130.3×91.6) sitting 2.5–8.5 cm over the threshold, on dims that repeat identically thousands of times — catalog/spec values, not per-parcel measurements — charged at ~50% incidence (a coin flip on identical declared dims = the physical box straddles the line). The "legitimate" label overstated; the bucket belongs in the same physical-audit scope as the contested zone.

**The lesson.** When a verdict decomposes into explained vs disputed buckets, the *explained* bucket inherits the epistemic status of the data that explains it — and declared catalog dims are a label, not a measurement (sibling of the populated-column-is-not-a-measurement and categorical-class-needs-physical-cross-check reflexes, applied to the bucket I was *confident* about, not the one I doubted). Run the falsifying drill (what are these, concretely? does the incidence pattern support the label?) on the explained bucket before presenting it as settled, especially when its euro figure anchors a recommendation.

**How to apply.** Any "X% explained / legitimate / matches the rule" claim: before presenting, pull the concrete membership (top cohorts, dims provenance, incidence) and check whether the explaining feature is a measurement or a spec value. If the membership concentrates in one borderline cohort, say so in the headline, not in the follow-up.
