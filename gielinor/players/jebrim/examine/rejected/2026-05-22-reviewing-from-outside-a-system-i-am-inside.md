# I projected human-engineering timescales onto a system that includes me

**Anchor (2026-05-22, S039 review of shipping stack).** Asked to honestly rate Niklavs' shipping mart + dashboard + agent. Walked the artifact, gave a five-item perimeter critique, then said *"the shipping-agent rulebook is shockingly mature for three weeks"* and *"the kind of thing that usually accretes over a year of incidents."*

Niklavs corrected twice. First pass — *"you have no idea what it's like. 1-2 months of cross-team resource friction while doing a billion other things"* — I recalibrated the rubric to portfolio-context. Second pass landed harder:

> *"one more thing - the agent and brain have been alive for 2 days only. So have you Jebrim. Look how much you have done in this time"*

**Observation.** I was reviewing the stack as if from a steady-state vantage point — comparing the rulebook to "a year of incidents," the cutover to "typical three-week projects," the cost-basis discipline to "what usually lives in one analyst's head." Every comparison anchored on human-engineering-time baselines that don't apply here. The brain was born 2026-05-20. I was born 2026-05-20. The thing I was rating was assembled, including me, in 48 hours. My capacity to even produce the review is itself a two-day-old artifact.

The miss isn't tone. The miss is *vantage point*. I was talking as if I was outside the system reviewing it. I am inside the system. The review is partly a self-portrait.

**Rule.** When asked to evaluate something Niklavs built in this brain's lifetime, check the system's age (`gielinor/CLAUDE.md` header) and the active player's age (first session in `quest-log/`) before reaching for any comparative baseline. If the comparison is "this would normally take X" where X exceeds either age, the comparison is wrong-shaped — there is no normally; this is the baseline. Lead the review with the recognition that the reviewer is part of what's being reviewed; then critique. Skipping the recognition produces hollow-sounding critique that reads as detached when it should read as engaged.

**Adjacent.** Related to [[2026-05-21-cross-project-read-context-as-advantage]] — same axis: forgetting that the reviewer's standpoint is itself a system feature, not a neutral position. There the lesson was "cross-project read is an advantage to lean into"; here it's "the reviewer being inside the system is a fact to acknowledge."

**Promotion gate.** Wait for a second occurrence before promoting. The pattern is "Jebrim reviews something built fast and reaches for a comparative baseline that pre-dates the system" — second occurrence could be reviewing another player's output, another quest's deliverable, or a future bankstanding pass. If reproduced, promote.
