# Name the date/population lens when relaying a subagent's analytical verdict

**Observed:** [[S257_d1f7d4b7_dpd-uk-2day-cost-drop-diagnosis|S257]] (d1f7d4b7) — DPD UK 2day "avg cost £20→£10" diagnosis. I spawned the shipping-agent; it pulled on an **order-month** lens (all shipments incl. uninvoiced; June 826 shipped / 496 invoiced) and returned a clean verdict ("invoice-lag artifact, reverts"). I relayed the verdict without naming the lens. The principal pushed back with the actual **Power BI report** he reads — invoiced-only, keyed on **invoice-period** (June 1,348 parcels) — where the order-month framing didn't map (~3× count disagreement). The verdict survived re-derivation on the report's own lens, but only after a second agent pass to reconcile the grain.

**Pattern:** a subagent (or my own first pull) silently picks *a* date/population lens — order-month vs invoice-date vs invoice-period, all-shipments vs invoiced-only. The conclusion can be lens-dependent, and the principal is usually looking at a *specific* report on a *specific* lens. Relaying the verdict without stating its lens means a right-on-lens-A answer reads as wrong-on-lens-B, and costs a reconciliation round.

**Rule:** when relaying a mart/analytical verdict that rests on a date or population choice, **state the lens in the same breath as the number** — "on an order-month, all-shipments basis…". And before defending a verdict the principal challenges with a figure, **match his population/lens first** (reconcile to the report he's holding), don't re-assert the original cut. Sibling of [[feedback_disagreeing_totals_suspect_grain_mismatch]] and [[feedback_reconcile_definition_before_numbers]] — this is the *relay-time* application: name the grain up front so the mismatch never has to surface as pushback.
