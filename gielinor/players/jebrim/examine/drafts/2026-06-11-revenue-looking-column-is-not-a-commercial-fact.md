# A revenue-looking column is not a commercial fact

**Anchor ([[S204_e9dbce2d_na-may-quota-breakdown|S204]]/e9dbce2d, 2026-06-11, NA May-quota breakdown):** I read `dw.sales_fact.shipping_amount_eur` on PicaAPI orders (May NA: €246.5k, ≈ the channel's €233.6k shipping cost) and built the deliverable's strongest claim on it — "API shipping is fully recharged to resellers, the quota rise is optical." Niklavs: "we do pay the shipping, so that point does not stand." The field is not recharge income; the magnitude match made the wrong story seductive.

**The rule (1–3 sentences):** A populated money column plus a plausible name is not evidence of a money *flow* — who pays whom is a commercial fact that lives in contracts/the principal's knowledge, not in column names. Before building a P&L argument on a revenue-looking field, verify what it represents (and who the counterparty is) with the principal or the field's documented lineage. Sibling of [[2026-06-09-populated-column-is-not-a-measurement]] — that one is provenance-of-measurement; this one is provenance-of-money-flow, and a near-equality to a cost figure is *not* verification (it's what made the error convincing).
