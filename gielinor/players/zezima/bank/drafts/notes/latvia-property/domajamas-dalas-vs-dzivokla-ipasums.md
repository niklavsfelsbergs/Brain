# Latvian apartment ownership: domājamās daļas vs dzīvokļa īpašums

**Claim.** When buying a Riga apartment, the single most important legal fact is *which
form of ownership* it is — and a buyer must read the wording for **what it modifies**.

- **Dzīvokļa īpašums** = a registered separate apartment (its own Land-Register section /
  *nodalījums*), governed by the *Dzīvokļa īpašuma likums*. You own the flat. This is the
  clean, financeable, normal case — buy without a second thought (on the ownership axis).
- **An apartment sold AS a *domājamā daļa*** (a notional/ideal share of a whole undivided
  building, *kopīpašums*, Civillikums 1067/1072) = you own an abstract fraction of the
  building, not the flat. Your right to occupy a specific apartment exists only via a
  *lietošanas kārtība* that binds future buyers **only if registered in the Zemesgrāmata**.
  Exposed to CL 1074 (any co-owner can force division → possible forced auction of the
  whole building), CL 1073 pre-emption on sale, financing is hard (Tier-1 banks resist;
  Altum mostly for *pusmāja*), resale is discounted/illiquid, and conversion to proper
  title needs **unanimous** consent of all co-owners. **The "time bomb" case** — worst
  when it's a share of a large multi-apartment building (e.g. 70 units → conversion
  effectively impossible, mortgage likely declined).

**The trap (verbatim-reading lesson).** *"Īpašuma sastāvā ir zemes gabala domājamās daļas"*
= ideal shares **of the land plot** — this is the NORMAL, healthy case: the apartment is a
proper *dzīvokļa īpašums* that also owns its share of the land (a plus — no forced ground-
rent). The phrase "domājamās daļas" appearing in a listing is NOT automatically the bad
case; check whether it modifies *the apartment* (bad) or *the land* (normal). A
*dzīvokļu īpašnieku biedrība* (apartment-owners' association) managing the building
confirms it's divided into proper units.

**Anchors.** S056 (2026-05-23). Two Riga apartments evaluated: #1 a ~70-unit building held
in domājamās daļas → walk (structural/legal danger); #2 Duntes 28 "Duntes Ozoli" where the
land-share wording initially read as co-ownership but verbatim re-read + the biedrība
confirmed a proper dzīvokļa īpašums.
