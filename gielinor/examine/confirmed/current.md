## The standing reflexes (cross-player, graduated to global)

Each graduated to the global layer because it recurs across more than one player / self-knowledge store — system-level, not character-specific.

### Verify the thing — don't trust the wiring
When asserting a guarantee holds, a measurement is valid, a capability exists, or a state is true: exercise it from the real entry point and watch it behave before claiming it. "The wiring looks right" is not evidence the wiring fires. The general form of a seven-instance family — do the forbidden thing and watch the guard catch it; confirm the metric measures the claim, not a confound; test the assumed limit before declaring something unverifiable; check the live source, not the fixture; treat a glob / `empty` result that underwrites a conclusion as a claim to cross-check (`ls` / `git ls-files`). → `2026-06-01-verify-the-thing-dont-trust-the-wiring` (parent of the family); child `2026-06-01-glob-results-are-claims-not-ground-truth`.

### Anchor to existing state before treating work as new
Before re-deriving, re-analysing, or asserting "this is open / absent / new," check the state that already exists — on disk, in the bank, in the git log, in context. Default to continuation until ground truth proves the work new. Met through *recognition* (is this known work?) on the reflective side and *verification* (is my state-claim true?) on the execution side — same reflex, two angles. The standing-rule form of the preamble's Recall-shaped lens. → `2026-05-30-anchor-to-existing-state-before-treating-work-as-new`

### Complete the cheap grounding precondition before substantive output
Before substantive output: (a) read the active context (a player's `keepsake/current.md` at minimum); (b) map continuation cues to existing work and confirm the referent before analysing on a novelty assumption. The Understanding/Plan preamble confirms the ask, not the prior context or the referent. Confirmed independently in two players. → `2026-05-25-grounding-precondition-before-substantive-output`
