# Parcel-audit / shipping-cost-recovery industry

As of: 2026-06-09

- Three overlapping tiers: (a) refund-recovery SMB SaaS (Refund Retriever, Share-A-Refund, 71lbs, AuditShipment - FedEx/UPS only, pure contingency); (b) full parcel-audit + intelligence SaaS, mid-market (Sifted, Reveel, ShipSigma, Lojistic, LateShipment, Enveyo); (c) enterprise managed-service / freight-audit-payment (Shipware, Green Mountain, Intelligent Audit, Trax, Transporeon, ConData).
- Contingency pricing is near-universal in tier (a): Refund Retriever 40-50% of refunds, Share-A-Refund 50%, LateShipment 35%. SaaS examples: Outvio from $4,500/yr, ShipScience $0.40/shipment. Tier (c) is custom retainer + gainshare blends.
- Vendor savings claims: audit alone ~1-5% of spend; full service incl. negotiation up to ~25-30% (ShipSigma). All self-reported.
- Central structural finding: the cluster is US- and FedEx/UPS-centric, built on those two carriers' money-back-guarantee regimes. EU carriers (DHL, DPD, GLS, Hermes, PostNL, etc.) have no equivalent automatable refund regime, so the US playbook does not transplant; claimed EU coverage is shallow invoice ingestion, not carrier-specific surcharge/zone/DIM logic.
- Deep re-rating against actuals is largely absent: tools detect billing errors and service failures, not line-level re-rates against the negotiated contract and alternative carriers on the shipper's own dims/weights/zones. "Benchmarking" against peer rates is the weaker substitute sold instead.
- The contingency model structurally caps depth - 35-50%-of-refunds economics reward volume of easy automatable claims, not the hard analytical work where the larger savings sit.
- Tender/RFP design exists only at the enterprise advisory tier at retainer pricing; out of reach for mid-market EU shippers.
- Net white space: EU multi-carrier deep re-rating on the shipper's own invoice actuals + tender design, delivered as analysis-grade work - the seam between US contingency SaaS and freight-first enterprise FAP firms. The EU-thinness conclusion is largely absence-of-evidence (inferred from uniform US framing); flagged for a targeted EU-startup search if decision-vital.
