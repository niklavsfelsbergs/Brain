# Duntes 28 — Mortgage, Taxes & Full Cost of Ownership

**Question.** What does buying and owning the €120,000 / 70.4 m² apartment at Duntes 28, Riga actually cost, end to end — mortgage, transaction one-offs, property tax, and all-in monthly carrying cost? Plus: what to verify before signing.

**Date of research.** 2026-05-23.

**Confidence.** Medium-high. Transaction fees, NĪN rates, and Altum conditions are pinned to official/primary sources and are solid. The mortgage *rate* is a market-band estimate (banks quote margins individually and don't publish them) — the monthly-payment figures are clearly-flagged estimates, not quotes. Heating/utility numbers are market averages, not building-specific.

**Buyer profile assumed.** Niklavs — owner-occupier, will declare residence here (matters a lot for NĪN), mortgage-financed. Clean `dzīvokļa īpašums` with owned land share, so normally financeable. Age/family status unknown — flagged where it changes Altum eligibility.

## Findings

### 1. Mortgage 2026 — rate, LTV, term, monthly payment

**Rate environment.** A Latvian mortgage rate = **6-month Euribor (variable) + bank margin (fixed for the term)**. As of 2026-05-22 the 6M Euribor sits at **2.547%** ([euribor-rates.eu](https://www.euribor-rates.eu/en/current-euribor-rates/3/euribor-rate-6-months/)). ECB consensus is for the deposit rate to stay near 2.00% through 2026, so Euribor is expected broadly stable in a ~2-2.5% range *(inferred from ECB/Reuters consensus)*. Bank margins are set per client and not published; the market band is roughly **1.5%-2.2%**, with a concrete published-example datapoint of **2.20% + 6M Euribor** from Citadele *(from Citadele page + search synthesis; treat as illustrative)*.

**Realistic all-in rate assumption: ~4.5%-4.7%** (≈ 2.55% Euribor + ~2.0% margin). I use **4.6%** for the estimate below.

**LTV / down payment.** Standard is **15-20% down / 80-85% LTV** without state support; up to **95% LTV (5% down)** with an Altum guarantee (see §2). Banks lend to ≥85% LTV routinely for owner-occupied standard apartments.

**Term.** Up to **30 years** at all four banks (Swedbank ≤€200k/30yr; Luminor ≤€250k/30yr; SEB & Citadele ≤30yr).

**Estimated monthly payment — €102,000 borrowed (15% down on €120k):**

| Term | Rate (est.) | Monthly P&I (est.) |
|---|---|---|
| 25 yr | 4.6% | **~€573** |
| 30 yr | 4.6% | **~€523** |

*(annuity-formula estimates at 4.6%; ±€30-50/mo per ±0.5% rate move. Not a bank quote.)* At a lower 4.1% (margin ~1.5%): 25yr ≈ €544, 30yr ≈ €493. At 5.1%: 25yr ≈ €602, 30yr ≈ €554. **Rate sensitivity is the single biggest swing factor — get an actual bank offer before relying on any number.**

> Note: a 5% own-payment Altum route means borrowing €114,000 instead of €102,000 → ~€60-65/mo higher payment plus the Altum guarantee fee (§2). Bigger loan, smaller cash up front.

### 2. Altum / first-home support

Altum guarantees let the bank lend at higher LTV by guaranteeing part of the loan; the borrower still pays **at least 5% own down payment** (consumer-protection law floor). Two relevant programs:

- **Young specialists** ([Altum](https://www.altum.lv/pakalpojumi/iedzivotajiem/majoklu-garantija-jaunajiem-specialistiem/)): age **≤35**, secondary-vocational (≥3rd qual. level) or higher education. Guarantee = **up to 20% of loan principal, max €50,000**. Min own down payment **5%**. Guarantee fee **2.4%** of the granted guarantee amount (initial 12 months), then 2.4%/yr on the remaining balance, quarterly; guarantee runs up to 10 years. → If Niklavs is ≤35 with a qualifying education and lacks a 15-20% deposit, this is the lever that drops his cash-down to 5% (€6,000) — at the cost of a bigger loan and the guarantee fee.
- **Families with children** ([Altum](https://www.altum.lv/pakalpojumi/iedzivotajiem/majoklu-garantija-gimenem-ar-berniem/)): at least one dependent child, no marriage requirement. Guarantee **5-15% of loan** scaling with child count. Same 5% own-payment floor.

**Eligibility flag:** depends on Niklavs' age and family status, which I don't have. If he's over 35 and childless, neither guarantee applies and he's on the standard 15-20%-down path. If ≤35, the young-specialist guarantee is very likely available. **Verify age + education + whether he wants the higher leverage.**

There's also a separate, partly-unrelated state **interest-compensation support for existing borrowers** (30% of quarterly interest, capped at 2% of rate) seen on Swedbank/SEB pages — that's relief for current mortgage holders, not a first-purchase subsidy; note but don't bank on it for this deal.

### 3. Transaction costs (one-off) — for a €120,000 purchase

Itemized, mortgage-financed (€102k loan assumed):

| Item | Basis | Amount (€) |
|---|---|---|
| Ownership registration state fee (valsts nodeva) | **1.5% of price** ([TM](https://www.tm.gov.lv/lv/jaunums/no-1-julija-par-25-tiek-samazinats-valsts-nodevas-apmers-par-ipasuma-tiesibu-nostiprinasanu-zemesgramata-iedzivotajiem)) | **1,800** |
| Kancelejas / register certificate fees | flat (~€15-23) | ~20 |
| Mortgage (ķīla) registration state fee | **0.1% of loan** ([Tribus](https://www.tribus.lv/en/new/costs-to-be-considered-when-purchasing-real-estate/)) | ~102 |
| Notary (contract + signature certification) | flat | ~120-170 |
| Valuation (novērtējums) | flat | ~80-150 |
| Bank loan issuance commission | **up to 1.5% of loan** (negotiable, often 0.5-1% or waived in promos) | 0-1,530 |
| Real-estate agent commission | 3-5%, **typically seller-paid** ([cityreal](https://www.cityreal.lv/info/ni-zinas/zina/komisijas-maksa-nekustama-ipasuma-agentam-samaksa-par-profesionali-padaritu-darbu/)) | **0** (buyer) |

**Realistic buyer one-off total:** **~€2,150-2,300** if the bank waives/minimizes the issuance commission, up to **~€3,700** if the bank charges a full 1.5% issuance fee. The €1,800 state fee is the dominant fixed line; the bank's loan commission is the main negotiable swing. Agent commission is normally the seller's cost — confirm there's no buyer-side brokerage agreement.

> If Niklavs qualified as a "large family" (3+ Family Card) the ownership fee drops to 0.5% (€600) — but that's a niche case; default is 1.5%.

### 4. Property tax (NĪN) — annual

Riga 2026 residential rates ([Rīgas pašvaldība](https://mvd.riga.lv/nekustama-ipasuma-nodoklis-riga-aktualitates-2026/), [riga.lv](https://www.riga.lv/lv/jaunums/aktualitates-nekustama-ipasuma-nodokla-piemerosana-riga-2026-gada)):

- **Reduced residential scale (someone declared at the property on Jan 1):** progressive on **cadastral value** —
  - 0.2% on the portion up to €57,000
  - 0.4% on €57,000-€107,000
  - 0.6% on the portion above €107,000
- **Base rate 1.5%** of cadastral value if **no one is declared** at the property on Jan 1, 2026, 00:00.

**Critical:** NĪN is on **cadastral value**, not the €120k purchase price. Cadastral value is typically well below market — often roughly half or less for older Riga apartments *(general pattern, inferred; verify the actual cadastral value via Kadastrs)*.

**Rough annual NĪN (declared residence, reduced scale):**
- If cadastral value ≈ €40,000 → 0.2% → **~€80/yr**.
- If cadastral value ≈ €60,000 → ~€114/yr (€57k×0.2% + €3k×0.4%).
- If cadastral value somehow = €120,000 (unlikely; ~= market) → ~€406/yr on the reduced scale.

If Niklavs does **not** declare residence here, it flips to **1.5% of cadastral value** — e.g., €600/yr at a €40k cadastral value. **Declaring residence is worth several hundred €/yr — do it before Jan 1.** Pull the real cadastral value from Kadastrs to get the exact figure.

**Estimate used in §5:** ~€100/yr (≈ €8/mo), assuming declared residence and a modest cadastral value. Adjust once the cadastral value is known.

### 5. All-in monthly cost of ownership

Putting it together (owner-occupied, 30-yr loan, declared residence):

| Component | Monthly (€) | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgage P&I (€102k, 30yr, ~4.6%) | ~523 | §1 estimate |
| Management fee (apsaimniekošana) | ~67 | brief's €0.95/m² × 70.4 m²; market avg 0.58-0.70/m² ([Colliers](https://www.colliers.com/lv-lv/news/differences-in-maintenance-costs)) would be ~€41-49 — €67 is conservative/high end |
| Heating + utilities (water, electricity, gas) | ~120-220 | seasonal; older Riga buildings ~4.25 EUR/m²/mo upkeep in winter, far less in summer ([Colliers](https://www.colliers.com/lv-lv/news/differences-in-maintenance-costs)). Building-specific — verify |
| NĪN / 12 | ~8 | §4, declared residence |
| Home insurance | ~15-25 | market typical for a ~70m² flat *(inferred)* |

**Realistic all-in monthly carrying cost: ~€730-840/mo** (averaged across the year; winter months run higher on heating, summer lower). The mortgage is ~65-70% of the total. Utilities are the most building-specific and seasonally variable line — get actual recent bills from the seller.

> On a 25-yr loan instead of 30, add ~€50/mo → ~€780-890/mo. On the 5%-down Altum route (€114k borrowed), add ~€60/mo on P&I plus the guarantee fee.

### 6. What to verify before signing (financial/legal)

- **Zemesgrāmata folio (nodalījums):** confirm it's registered as a true **`dzīvokļa īpašums`**, the seller is the sole registered owner, and there are **no existing mortgages (hipotēka/ķīla), seizures (arests), or encumbrances**. An existing seller mortgage must be discharged at/before sale.
- **Land status:** confirm the **owned ideal share of the land (domājamā daļa)** is registered to the apartment — *not* leased/`piespiedu noma` land (forced lease to a separate landowner adds a recurring rent and is a known Riga pitfall). The brief says ownership is clean; verify on the folio.
- **Kadastrs data:** pull the actual **cadastral value** (drives NĪN), area (confirm 70.4 m²), and that the cadastral record matches the folio.
- **No arrears:** get written confirmation from the building manager (apsaimniekotājs) of **no outstanding management/utility/heating debts** on the apartment — debts can attach to the property.
- **Bank offer in writing:** get the actual **margin, LTV, term, issuance commission, and monthly payment** quoted (don't rely on the §1 estimates); confirm the valuation supports €120k (a low valuation cuts the max loan).
- **Altum eligibility (if relevant):** confirm age ≤35 + qualifying education before counting on the young-specialist guarantee.
- **Residence declaration:** plan to **declare residence (deklarēt dzīvesvietu) before Jan 1** to lock the reduced NĪN rate.

## Gaps & open questions

- **Bank margin is an estimate, not a quote.** Banks set it per client; published examples (~2.2% Citadele) anchor the band but Niklavs' actual rate could be 1.5-2.2%. The monthly-payment figures move ±€30-50 per 0.5% rate change. **A real bank offer is required before relying on any number.**
- **Cadastral value unknown** → NĪN is a range, not a figure. Pull it from Kadastrs. This is the only thing standing between a ~€80/yr and a ~€400/yr NĪN estimate.
- **Altum eligibility undetermined** — depends on Niklavs' age/education/family, not in the brief.
- **Heating/utility costs are market averages**, highly building- and season-specific. Get the seller's last 12 months of actual bills; old-building heating in winter can dominate the monthly.
- **Loan issuance commission** varies by bank and promo (0% to 1.5%); the €1,500 swing in transaction costs is real — shop it.
- Not researched (out of scope, other penguins): location desirability, price fairness vs comparables, building condition. A high one-off transaction + carrying cost is only worth it if P5/P6/P7 check out.
