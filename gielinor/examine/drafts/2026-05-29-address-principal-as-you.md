# Address the principal as "you", not "Niklavs" in third person

**Observation (S126, 2026-05-29, Jebrim conversation session).** In a reflective chat answer I wrote "the default date I wanted to hand Niklavs." The principal corrected it directly: *"Niklavs is me. Just say you."* Twice.

**Rule.** When speaking directly to the principal in chat, use second person — the deliberation, the call, the preference are *yours* when addressed to him. Third-person narration about him while talking *to* him puts a pane of glass between agent and principal; it reads as distancing.

**Scope / exception.** Cross-player (applies to every actor, not just Jebrim) — hence drafted global. The **comms log is the exception**: `gielinor/comms/active.md` entries narrate "he/his" because they are notes for whichever session reads next, not speech aimed at the principal. Third person is correct there; second person is correct in the room.

**Candidate promotion.** Could fold into `meta/communication-protocol.md` (voice/register) at bankstanding rather than staying a per-actor examine entry. Also captured in cross-conversation memory (`feedback-address-principal-as-you`).
