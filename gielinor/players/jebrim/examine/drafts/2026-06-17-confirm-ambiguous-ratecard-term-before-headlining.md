# Confirm an ambiguous rate-card term before headlining a number off it

**Observation ([[S261_c2f15e55_uk-yodel-oog-cap-correction|S261]], 2026-06-17, UK Yodel).** The Yodel card's fuel line read *"8% discount of the published rate."* I modelled it three different ways across the session before it was pinned:
1. First as a **−8% discount on base** (a *cost reduction*) — wrong sign entirely.
2. Then, after fetching the fuel page (published 18%), as **18% × 0.92 = 16.56%** (relative discount) — a *surcharge*, sign fixed but magnitude wrong.
3. Principal corrected to **18% − 8% = 10%** (percentage-points). The headline Yodel cost moved £404K → £385K between readings 2 and 3.

Two independent ambiguities hid in one short phrase: **discount-vs-surcharge** (does it reduce or add?) and **relative-vs-percentage-points** (8% *of* the rate, or 8 points *off* it?). Each swung the number materially.

**Why it matters.** I published a headline (£404K) off reading 2 before it was confirmed. The number was load-bearing for the decision. A rate-card phrase that admits >1 quantitative reading is a *hypothesis*, not a value — sibling to [[2026-06-09-read-the-rate-type-column-not-just-the-value]] and the classify-doc-before-reading-a-date reflex.

**How to apply.** When a rate/discount/surcharge term has more than one plausible reading AND it materially moves a headline: surface *both* readings explicitly at the Plan/assumption line, model it as a flagged parameter, and get the principal/carrier to confirm **before** stating a headline. Don't let the first plausible reading harden into "the number." (I did flag it as soft + parametrised the engine — that's what made the correction cheap; the miss was leading with a figure off the unconfirmed reading.)
