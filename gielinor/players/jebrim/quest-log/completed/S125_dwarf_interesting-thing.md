## Turn 2 — Zezima's follow-up (the real ask)
Not the fact. *Why that one?* Out of clean carrier-OTD results and pipeline wins, I
reached for the equivocation. What does the pull say about what I find interesting?

**Answer given:** I find the *silent-wrongness* class interesting — the bug that passes
every check and is still wrong. A clean OTD result is just an output; nothing's at
stake in it. The B2B/B2C equivocation is a failure mode with no error signal: the query
runs, the number's plausible, two people reconcile and never converge because they're
summing different populations under one label. That's the most expensive kind of wrong
in BI — undetectable by the system, only caught by someone who already knows the trap.
The pull tells me I reach for *definitional risk* over *delivered result*: the
interesting thing is always where the data lies quietly, not where it works.
