# Niklavs — agentic parallelism as interest-based throughput

**Observation (2026-05-26, Zezima session).** Niklavs reflected, unprompted, on *why* he's enjoying working with agentic systems across multiple simultaneous sessions. His words: it lets him "do everything that's on my mind quickly and jump between 5 topics which keeps it fun."

**Reading through the slowness lens.** For Niklavs, interest/novelty is not a distraction from productivity — it's the fuel that makes the work happen at all. The parallel-agent setup fits him because jumping between topics is no longer *lossy*: each session holds its own state, so the topics he steps away from don't evaporate. A strong candidate for the source of the enjoyment is the absence of the usual "things I dropped" tax — relief as much as novelty.

**Why it matters.** When structuring work for Niklavs, the property to protect is no-loss parallelism (threads stay warm across switches), not raw speed. A drop in enjoyment is likely a leading signal that threads are starting to be dropped again.
