## Why the WALK stands ([[S056_e5df54a2_apartment-domajamas-dalas-research|S056]] T9, P2/P4)

The reasons were **structural**, untouched by "4k exist" or "2 years looking":
1. **Financing** — a bare 1/70 ideal share is what Tier-1 banks won't mortgage; Altum is
   *pusmāja*-shaped. If no bank lends, his mortgage-financed plan becomes all-cash = a different

3. **CL 1074 forced-division + creditor-auction exposure**, multiplied across 70 owners — never
   fully goes away short of conversion (which won't come).

## Turn 6 — "what are the absolute risks?" (cut-through, harvest-worthy)

He pushed: people DO buy these, lots of buildings are in this state — give me the real risk floor,
de-hedged. Answered with the honest model:
- **Most owners are fine** — the form is paperwork, not daily life; a functioning building +
  registered use-arrangement lives like a normal flat.
- **Risk floor ranked by likelihood-to-touch-him:** (1) near-certain *if he sells* — liquidity +
  permanent ~⅓ discount (a bounded cost, captured on entry); (2) likely-over-time but NOT a share
  risk — major-repair money on a 1913 / 57%-depreciated building (true of any old building);
  (3) possible/mostly-harmless — stranger co-owner via a neighbour's debt (registered arrangement
  neutralises it); (4) **rare but the real one** — forced division (CL 1074) → building auctioned,
  bought out at ~70–75%, loses the *home* not the money. Low odds in a functioning 81-owner
  building; never fully gone short of conversion (won't happen).
- **The one categorical difference:** titled = no one can ever force you out; share = law always
  technically permits it. #4 is a difference in *kind*; everything else is degree. Accept it on
  purpose.
- **"People are fine" isn't random:** the fine cases are the checked ones (registered arrangement,
  functioning building, financing exists). The gates ARE the filter between fine and burned — not
  lawyer-fluff. Blanket "avoid" is calibrated to the median *unchecked* flat; a checked functioning
  one usually sits in the fine bucket.

This framing ("categorical vs degree"; "the gates are the filter"; "discount captured on entry but
upside capped") is the strongest harvest candidate for `bank/notes/latvia-property/`.
