## The "lose if I sold after a year" frame (bounded-downside calc)

Niklavs's own way in: he spends ~€6k/yr on rent (100% unrecoverable), so he wants the worst case of
"buy it, hate it, sell in a year" priced against that.

- **Certain friction (round-trip in 1 yr):** buy one-offs ~€2.5–3.5k (1.5% state duty dominates +
  notary + mortgage setup); sell ~€4–5k (agent ≈3%+VAT — budget it; a *share* needs the reach) +
  small CGT on any gain (a 1-yr sale **forfeits the 5-yr primary-home PIT exemption**, so a gain is
  taxed ~20%, ~€1–2k if sold at profit, €0 if not). **≈ €7–9k of pure friction.**
- **Asset move (swings it):** market rising ~7–10%/yr → ~€8k paper gain on ~€110k of real estate
  **largely cancels the friction.** So **likely case (rising mkt, unhurried sale): net loss
  ~break-even to a few €k — *less than a year of rent.*** Flat market: ~€7k (friction only) ≈ one
  year of rent. **Bad case (soft mkt + *forced fast* share sale, thin pool, deep cut): €12–15k** ≈
  2–2.5 yrs of rent — and this is the **one scenario the share genuinely worsens.**
- **Frame:** worst *realistic* "tried it, bailed at 1yr" ≈ 1–2.5 yrs of rent, likely ~1 — except he
  *owned* it and held the option to stay; rent recovers nothing. **1 year is the worst horizon**
  (fixed costs unamortised + no CGT exemption); a 3–5yr hold swings hard toward buying and the share
  friction nearly vanishes. He controls the bad case: don't overpay on entry, don't get forced to
  sell fast.
