# S056_p1 — Penguin research: domājamā daļa legal structure

**Player:** Zezima | **Role:** penguin (p1) | **Date:** 2026-05-23

**Brief:** Under Latvian Civillikums, what does owning a *domājamā daļa* (ideal share of
co-owned property, kopīpašums) mean for a buyer who wants to legally occupy one specific
apartment — vs owning a registered *dzīvokļa īpašums*. Focus: (1) lietošanas kārtība and
whether it binds successors/new buyers; (2) right of any co-owner to demand division
(kopīpašuma izbeigšana); (3) pirmpirkuma tiesības on share sale. Cite Civillikums articles.

- T3: Fetched primary + explainers. Key findings locked:
  * CL 1067 = ideal-share def (verbatim); CL 1072 = each share belongs solely to its owner;
    CL 1068 = disposal of common thing needs ALL co-owners' consent (verbatim).
  * §1 lietošanas kārtība: buyer does NOT auto-get a flat; right to occupy one flat exists
    only via usage arrangement. REGISTERED in zemesgrāmata → binds successors/new buyers
    (verbatim 2017 quote: "rēķināties ar to arī gadījumā, ja viņu vietā ... kļūst cita
    persona"). UNREGISTERED → contractual, binds only signatories (inferred; medium conf).
    Court can impose arrangement if co-owners disagree.
  * §2 division: CL 1074 — any co-owner may demand division at any time (verbatim); Supreme
    Court: it's a lietu tiesība another co-owner can't block. CL 1075 forms: real division /
    buyout w/ compensation / AUCTION sale / (2022) split into dzīvokļu īpašumi. 2022 reform:
    5-yr bar (amended CL 1074 pt 2) w/ broad svarīgs-iemesls exceptions; new CL 1074.1
    closed-auction removal of misbehaving co-owner. Biggest occupant risk = forced-out via
    auction with compensation.
  * §3 pirmpirkuma: CL 1073 — 2-month first-refusal on sale to a NON-co-owner only; not on
    sales between co-owners; not on gifts; violation → izpirkuma tiesība (CL 1381 ff.).
