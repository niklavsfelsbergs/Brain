# Reconcile a model against raw invoice lines, not a derived/pre-bucketed cost column

**Observed ([[S279_6fbdcee1_orwo-tender-roadmap-and-ups-base-validation|S279]], 6fbdcee1).** I trust-gated the UPS rate-card model against the mart's `base_rate_eur` bucket and reported a scary "50–85% rate-card gap — modeled base €2.92–3.61 vs invoiced base €5.41." Tracing to the **silver charge lines** dissolved it: the actual forward-freight line (`Dom. Standard`) matches the contract card *to the cent* by weight band (€3.26/€4.14/€5.77 vs card). The €5.41 "actual base" was the **mart bucket**, which sums across the ~3.8 consolidated shipment rows per tracking *and* bundles `Rückholservice`/service lines + the mart's RTS redistribution. It is a derived analytics measure, not the contract's forward weight×zone base.

**Lesson.** A derived/pre-bucketed cost column (a gold-mart bucket, an allocated total) is **not** a valid reconciliation target for a rate-card model — it's been consolidated, bundled, and redistributed away from the carrier's own line structure. To validate a reprice model, gate it against the **raw invoice charge lines** at their native grain, where the base/fuel/surcharge split is the carrier's own. The mart is right for coverage/trends; wrong as the "what did the base cost" truth.

**How to apply.** Before declaring a model-vs-actual gap, confirm the "actual" is the *same construct* as the model output. If it's a bucket/rollup, drop to the source lines first. Sibling of [[reconcile-definition-before-numbers]] and the mart-not-accounting-complete note — a same-labeled figure (`base`) from two layers is usually two different things. Decision logged in `findings.md`: ORWO tender prices on **invoice lines only**, mart cost dropped.
