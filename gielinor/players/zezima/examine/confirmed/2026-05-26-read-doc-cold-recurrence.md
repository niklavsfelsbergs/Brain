**Observation (S095, 2026-05-26).** Niklavs opened with "I am *once again* considering the Gertrudes 113 apartment" and uploaded the Zemesgrāmata folio. I treated it as a fresh document and re-derived the whole analysis from scratch — only several turns later (going to save) did I discover Zezima already held a full **S066 deep-dive of this exact flat** (research + bank notes + synthesis verdict), and that the uploaded folio was precisely **Gate 2** the S066 verdict had told Niklavs to close. Before catching it I'd given him a breezier read ("I overweighted the risk", "fair, maybe a touch keen") than the careful prior conclusion.

**Why it matters / pattern.** This is a **recurrence** of an already-recorded lesson — [[feedback_anchor_referent_before_analyzing]] (memory, born S066 — *the same flat*) and [[feedback_check_own_memory_before_working_repo]] (memory, S076). A continuation cue ("once again", "again", "the X I prepared") means the subject is already known; the first move is to read my own bank/research/quest-log, place the artifact in prior work, confirm identity, THEN analyse. The lesson sitting in memory did **not** prevent the slip — which is the signal worth noting: thrice-seen now (S066, S076, S095), so the failure isn't *capture*, it's that nothing makes "check my own notes first" actually fire when a session opens with a continuation cue + an artifact. (Candidate for a respawn-step or hook fix — surface to Guthix/bankstanding, not just another draft.)

**How to apply.** On any session opening with a continuation cue and/or an uploaded artifact in a player's namespace: BEFORE analysing, glob/read that player's bank + research + quest-log for the subject; map the artifact to where prior work left off; confirm identity; only then proceed. Treat "this is new" as a hypothesis to disprove, not a default.
