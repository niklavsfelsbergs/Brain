# Before adding a cost to a selection comparison, check whether alternatives already embed it

**Observation ([[S172_df374cef_dhl-truck-cost-warenpost-kleinpaket|S172]], 2026-06-09).** Wiring the €0.46 truck cost into the dhl_paket engine, I hit a fork: add it to the cheapest-eligible **selection** comparison, or only to the final **total**? I recommended total-only — worried that adding it to Warenpost/Kleinpaket but not Paket would bias the Warenpost→Paket selection on an asymmetrically-modelled cost. Niklavs corrected: **Paket DE/Intl linehaul is bundled in their rate-card prices**; Warenpost/Kleinpaket linehaul is not. So adding €0.46 to the latter doesn't create asymmetry — it **removes** it, putting all services on the same all-in basis. Therefore it belongs in selection.

**Rule.** When deciding whether a separately-sized cost belongs in a *comparison* (not just the total), first establish whether the **alternatives already embed the equivalent cost** (rate-card-inclusive vs separately-billed). If they do, adding it to the un-embedded option is what makes the comparison fair — exclude-from-selection would be the bias, not include. My instinct inverted the asymmetry because I didn't check what the rate card already contained. Read what's *in* the competing prices before judging a comparison unfair.
