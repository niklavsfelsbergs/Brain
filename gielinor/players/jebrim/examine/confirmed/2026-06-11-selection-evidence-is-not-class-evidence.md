# Selection evidence is not class evidence

**Anchor (S196/5733cb1d, T20–T21, 2026-06-11).** Q06 reported "CUSTOM_OVERSIZED avg €141.67"
and I wrote "UPS-billed oversize fees corroborate the dims are real — not a template artifact."
Niklavs challenged the average ("I don't see that in invoices") → the €141.67 was the 72-parcel
Hermes-won tail of 3,880 (class avg €8.21). Then the dims check: fee-hit and no-fee parcels have
IDENTICAL recorded dims (L+girth ~312 both, p95 314) — if recorded dims were real, UPS's >300cm
rule would fine ~all of them; it fines 2%. My corroboration claim was half-wrong: the fees prove
the SELECTED 72 are physically big; they prove nothing about the class's recorded dims — which
turned out template-like.

**Rule.** Two halves: (1) never report a slice average without naming the selection mechanism
that produced the slice (the selected-by-cost tail of a class is not the class); (2) evidence
that validates the selected items does not validate the attribute for the population — ask
"what would this evidence look like on the NON-selected rows?" (here: no-fee parcels with
identical dims = the tell).
