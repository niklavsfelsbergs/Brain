## Findings

### 1. The project "Duntes Ozoli"

Built/commissioned by **SIA "Duntes Ozoli"**, architect **Viktors Ugrimovs (SIA "LPL")**, general contractor **SIA "Budimex Latvia"** (the Latvian arm of the large Polish builder Budimex). Completed **autumn 2006**; actively marketed through **2007** — which reconciles the listing's "2007" with the 2006 completion date (cityreal, apollo.lv).

Scale and form: a distinctive cascading structure of **three connected sections (7 / 13 / 16 floors)**, tallest reaching **~50 m**, with **8 descending terraces**, **217 apartments** (46–307 m²), an **~1.85 ha / 18,510 m² landscaped territory**, **87 underground + 96 above-ground parking** spaces, **24h physical security + video surveillance**, fire-safety systems, children's playground and sports court (cityreal, apollo.lv, lsm.lv). The marketing copy (apollo.lv, 2007) positioned it as premium: "only the highest-quality materials," parquet, sanitary equipment "from the best manufacturers," large south-facing terraces with views to the Vanšu Bridge.

**Reputation — the load-bearing nuance.** The project is *not* uncontroversially well-regarded. Two flags:

- It **won the 2008 "Bietes balva" ("Beetroot Award")** — an **anti-prize** run by the architecture portal A4D for *architectural failures*, not an honour (tvnet.lv, 2008-04-25). The jury called the building "very aggressive, built on a hill, dominating its surroundings… disturbingly emphasising its omnipresent existence," with an "awkward, irregular, contorted volume" and a facade that renders it "anonymously impersonal — a building that could exist in any Russian city or Mediterranean resort." This is an *aesthetic/urban-fit* criticism, not a structural or build-quality one — important to keep separate. It says the building is divisive to look at and dominant in its setting; it does not say it's badly built.
- A **dižozols (protected great oak) was felled** beside the building under a Nature Protection Agency permit, which drew public objection (lsm.lv). Reputational/PR, not a defect.

**Developer is liquidated.** SIA "Duntes Ozoli" (reg. 40003369164) is **liquidated** (firmas.lv / Lursoft). For a 2006 building this is normal — single-purpose project SPVs are routinely wound up after a development sells out — and the original construction warranties (typically expiring well before now) are long past. The practical consequence: **there is no developer entity to pursue for latent construction defects.** Recourse for any building-wide defect now runs through the owners' community and whatever the building's own technical documentation and reserve fund support.

### 2. Construction type and 2006/2007-era quality

**Material discrepancy — flag for the buyer.** The listing describes the building as **brick ("ķieģeļu")**. The independent building aggregator viszinis.lv classifies it as **monolithic reinforced concrete ("monolītais dzelzsbetons / dzelzsbetona konstrukcijas")** (viszinis.lv). These are not the same thing, and for a 16-storey 2006 tower a **monolithic reinforced-concrete frame is far more plausible** than load-bearing brick *(inference from building height + era — high-rises of this period in Riga are RC-frame; brick is typically a facade/infill cladding)*. The likely reality: an RC frame with brick/masonry infill or facade, which a listing may loosely call a "brick house." **A buyer should confirm the actual structural system from the building's technical passport / energy certificate** rather than relying on the listing word.

What to expect from a 2006-era Riga new-build generally *(synthesis, partly inferred)*:

- **Build quality**: substantially better than Soviet-era serial stock — RC frame, better thermal envelope, modern plumbing/wiring, individual heat metering. But it predates the post-2010 energy-code tightening, so it is **not** an A/B-class energy building (see §3).
- **Common defects to inspect at ~20 years old**: facade sealant/joint degradation and any cladding water-ingress; flat-roof / terrace membrane condition (this building has many terraces — terrace waterproofing is a known weak point and a potentially expensive one); balcony/loggia waterproofing and railings; ventilation (mechanical systems of this era may be tiring or noisy); first-generation lifts (3 sections, 16 floors → lift refurbishment is a foreseeable major cost); underground-garage waterproofing and drainage. *(These are the standard 15–25-year wear items for an RC high-rise; inspect, don't assume.)*
- viszinis.lv reports the building in **"ļoti labā tehniskā stāvoklī" (very good technical condition) as of 2026** — encouraging, but it's an aggregator label, not an inspection.

### 3. Energy efficiency, heating & running costs

**Energy class C.** viszinis.lv lists the building at **Class C** with **73.1 kWh/m²/yr** for heating — "21% above similar buildings" on that platform. Against the Latvian brackets (likumi.lv): **A ≤40, B 40–60, C 60–80 kWh/m²/yr** for residential heating. So 73.1 sits **firmly in C, near the top of the band** — typical for a mid-2000s build, clearly better than Soviet/1990s stock (D/E = 80–150 kWh/m²/yr per Swedbank), but **well short of a modern A/B building** (which would roughly halve heating energy; an energy retrofit can cut heating/hot-water cost 25–50% per Swedbank — but that's the gap, not a plan for this building).

**Realistic running costs for ~70 m².** Heating is **district heating (AS "Rīgas Siltums")**; tariff **83.01 €/MWh (excl. VAT) from 2025-10-01** (rs.lv). Two convergent estimates:

- **Top-down from viszinis.lv** for this building: heating **~0.90 €/m²/mo** averaged over the heating season → **~€63/mo over the heating season for 70 m²**, but this is a season-average; *peak winter months run higher than the average*. Total utilities listed at **2.30 €/m² (summer) / 3.60 €/m² (winter)** → roughly **€160/mo summer to €250/mo winter all-in for 70 m²** (this figure bundles heating, water, hot water, common-area energy, etc.).
- **Cross-check (energija-g.lv / riga.lv):** a 2-room apartment in a **new or renovated** building runs **~€45–55/mo heating in the coldest months**, vs **€70–90/mo** in an unrenovated leaky building. For 70 m² (a 2–3 room flat) in this Class-C building, **expect coldest-month heating in roughly the €55–75 range, and a full winter all-in (heating + water + management) in the ~€200–250/mo range** *(blended estimate from the two sources; the exact figure depends on the apartment's floor, orientation, and the all-terraces exposure)*.

**Is €0.95/m² management low/normal/high?** For a managed building with security + video + landscaped grounds, **it is normal-to-slightly-high but justified by the service level**, not a red flag.
- Colliers (2022 data, via search snippet — *flagged: WebFetch was blocked, figure from index*): **new/renovated buildings average ~2.95 €/m²/yr** upkeep vs **~4.25 €/m²/yr for older buildings**, with new builds carrying only **~0.13 €/m² for reserves** and **more often paying extra for security, video, gardening**. Note the Colliers "2.95 €/m²/**yr**" is an apples-to-different-units figure from the listing's "0.95 €/m²/**month** (excl. VAT)"; the listing's monthly fee (~€67/mo for 70 m², ~€800/yr) is on the **higher side because it bundles the premium services** (24h guard, video, large grounds) that Colliers notes new projects pay extra for. So: **the fee is consistent with a serviced premium building, not an outlier.** Confirm whether the €0.95 is *management only* or *includes the reserve-fund contribution* — that materially changes how to read it.

### 4. Management by an owners' association (biedrība)

The listing says the building is run by a **dzīvokļu īpašnieku biedrība** (apartment owners' association) rather than a professional manager. Trade-offs (kodols.lv, LV portāls):

**Pros:** direct owner control and **financial transparency**; **faster response** to repairs and complaints; freedom to negotiate the building's own supplier contracts; money stays in-house rather than feeding a management firm's margin.

**Cons / risks:** board members **often lack professional property-management skills**; it is **time-intensive** and hard to combine with full-time jobs (quality can hinge on a few volunteers); **not automatically cheaper** — competent management work still has to be paid for. The recurring expert line: success depends on **residents' sustained willingness to participate** — a biedrība with an engaged, competent board can outperform a professional manager; a disengaged one can underfund and defer maintenance.

**What this means for a 200+-apartment, 3-section, 16-storey building:** self-management at *this* scale is demanding (lifts, garage, fire systems, large facade/terrace envelope). The biedrība's **competence and the health of its reserve fund are the single biggest variable** in whether the building stays sound and whether the buyer faces surprise special levies.

### 5. Reserve fund (uzkrājuma fonds) — the buyer's central question

The reserve fund is the building's "security cushion" for planned and emergency major repairs (building.lv, Bonava expert A. Vindels). Key points:

- **Typical contribution: 0.10–0.20 €/m²/mo**, but it *must* be sized to the building's actual condition and forthcoming repairs — a flat rate is a warning sign of under-planning (building.lv, lvportals).
- **If a building doesn't build the fund from day one, it "enters a risk zone" once warranties expire** — exactly the situation of a 2006 building whose developer is liquidated. Underfunding forces **unexpected special levies (vienreizēji maksājumi)** on owners when a big-ticket item (roof, facade, lifts, terrace membranes) comes due.
- **The fund must not be raided to cover debts** or non-emergency, un-voted repairs — that's expressly improper (lvportals). A fund being drained for arrears is a red flag.

**Buyer must request, in writing:** current total reserve balance, what's been spent and on what, the monthly contribution rate, and the **list of planned major repairs for the coming years** with whether they're funded or will trigger a levy.

### 6. Due-diligence checklist for the building itself

Distilled from PTAC (official), building.lv, kodols.lv, and the BIS-system guidance:

**Documents / registries to pull:**
- **Ownership & encumbrances** via **zemesgramata.lv** (mortgages, liens, debts on the specific apartment).
- **Building status & structural assessments** via **bis.gov.lv** (Būvniecības informācijas sistēma) — confirm legal status, any unauthorised construction, structural-integrity studies, and the **energy certificate register** (get the actual energy class/figure, not the listing's claim).
- **Heating efficiency** for Rīgas Siltums-connected buildings via **georiga.lv**.
- **Management entity** — check the **dzīvojamo māju pārvaldnieku reģistrs**; confirm whether it's truly a biedrība and who chairs it.

**Questions to the biedrība / seller:**
- **Reserve fund**: balance, contribution rate, history of spend, planned repairs and whether funded (see §5).
- **Building debt / arrears**: any collective debt to Rīgas Siltums, water, waste; request the utility-payment history for the apartment to confirm no arrears transfer with it.
- **Planned / recent major works**: facade, roof, terrace waterproofing, lifts, garage, ventilation — done, scheduled, or looming. Request **owners'-community meeting minutes (kopības lēmumi)** for the last 2–3 years — these reveal disputes, deferred maintenance, and pending levies.
- **What the €0.95/m² covers** — management only, or management + reserve + which services.

**Physical inspection (ideally with a hired specialist before signing the acceptance act):**
- Apartment: walls, floors, ceilings, electrical, plumbing.
- Common/structural: facade joints & cladding, **flat-roof and terrace membranes** (this building's many terraces are the highest-risk water-ingress item), balcony/loggia waterproofing, lift age, garage waterproofing, ventilation noise/condition.
- Confirm **individual heat meters + radiator regulators** are actually present and functioning (the listing's selling point — verify, since it directly controls the heating bill).

**Contract watch-outs (PTAC):** avoid clauses that waive the buyer's right to raise quality complaints; get all price components and any VAT/registration fees specified; since the **developer is liquidated**, don't expect any residual new-build warranty — treat it as a fully second-hand purchase.

- **Whether €0.95/m² includes the reserve contribution** is unknown — changes whether the fee is "normal" or "high."
- **Colliers figures came from the search index, not a direct fetch** (the page 403'd twice on WebFetch). The 2.95/4.25 €/m²/yr and "3× lower heating" figures are 2022-dated and should be re-confirmed if precision matters.
- **No independent inspection report or recent meeting minutes** were available online — by design, these are private to the building.
- **Heating cost estimates are blended/season-averaged**; the apartment-specific figure depends on floor, orientation, and terrace exposure, and on the actual coldest-month consumption rather than the season average viszinis.lv reports.
- The "Bietes balva" and oak-felling items are **reputational/aesthetic**, not structural — no evidence found of structural defects, litigation, or manager scandals specific to this building (a targeted search for disputes returned nothing).
