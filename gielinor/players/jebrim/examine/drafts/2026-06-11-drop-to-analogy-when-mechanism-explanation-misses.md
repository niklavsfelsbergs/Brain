# Drop to a concrete analogy when a mechanism explanation misses twice

**Observation ([[S196_5733cb1d_eu-tender-result-investigation|S196]] round 2, 2026-06-11, T29–T30
exchange).** Explaining why a loss-booking move appears in the routing, I gave two technically
precise answers (decision-basis vs reporting-basis, March anchor vs Q1 actuals, per-parcel
distributions). Niklavs answered "nope I still dont get it. Explain simply." The third attempt —
a gym-membership price-rise analogy plus "the two columns answer two different questions" —
landed immediately ("okay, I got it").

**The rule.** When a numeric-mechanism explanation doesn't land, the fix is not more precision —
it's a register switch: name the two questions being conflated, then carry the mechanism in a
concrete everyday analogy. Adding detail to a missed explanation compounds the miss; the second
"I don't get it" should trigger the switch automatically, before being asked to simplify.
