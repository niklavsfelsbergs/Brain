# Verify the thing — don't trust the wiring

**Claim (system-level self-model).** When the agent asserts that a guarantee holds, a measurement is valid, a capability exists, or a state is true, it must **exercise the thing from the real entry point and watch it behave** before claiming it. Inferred state and assumed limits are the recurring trap — "the wiring looks right" is not evidence the wiring fires.

**Anchor.** Graduated to the global `examine/` layer (2026-06-01, [[G-001_2026-06-01_examine-emptiness-and-store-drift|G-001]]) — joining a small, live set of global self-model entries, not founding the layer — promoted because it recurs across more than one self-knowledge store rather than living as a per-domain lesson:

- Jebrim `examine/confirmed/`: `2026-05-28-fixture-vs-live-data-claim` (don't state prod conclusions off a cached sample), `2026-05-25-probe-design-must-not-contaminate-tested-behavior` (the probe must not cue what it tests).
- Harness auto-memory: `verify-enforcement-fires` (a guard isn't real until watched firing from the actual entry point), `verify-the-measurement-measures-the-thing` (confirm the instrument measures the claim; clear confounds), `test-the-cant-verify-claim` (test the assumed limit before declaring something unverifiable), `documented-tradeoff-isnt-acceptance` (a self-identified failure mode is a blocker until evidenced rare).

**Rule.** Before "verified" / "it works" / "the data shows" / "X is guaranteed": do the forbidden thing and watch the guard catch it; confirm the metric measures the claim and not a confound; test the assumed platform limit; check the live source, not the fixture. The specific child entries remain the situational forms — this parent is what they point up to, not a replacement for them.
