## 3. Adjustment walk — from comps to a fair value for flat 41

Start from the **east-Centre renovated *titled* asking** level and walk down/up to flat-41 reality.

**(a) Base: renovated 2-room, east-Centre, titled, asking.** Drop the prestige-belt comps; the
Avoti-belt renovated titled asking centres ~**€1,800–2,200/m²** (Matīsa €2,178; mid-street renovated
titled stock; above the €1,400–1,600 floor of the weakest/wood-frame stock, below the €2,950+ prestige
belt). Call it **~€2,000/m² titled asking** as the working base for a *good* Avoti-belt renovated
2-room. Note this is already a touch *below* the subject's €2,073 ask.

**(b) Asking → achieved.** Centre runs ~10% asking-to-achieved (VZD anchor). €2,000 ask →
**~€1,800/m² achieved titled.** (Shares plausibly cut deeper to close — flagged, not quantified.)

**(c) Share discount (GIVEN ~a third; shallow end here).** Prior work places flat 41 at the
**shallow** end of the 25–45% band — registered *lietošanas kārtība*, functioning *biedrība*,
demonstrably bank-financed building (5 banks lent here in ~18 months per the folio). Apply ~**20%**
(shallow-end, because the file is unusually clean for a share) to the **titled achieved** base:
€1,800 × 0.80 ≈ **€1,440/m² achieved**, or against titled *asking* €2,000 × ~0.75 ≈ **€1,500/m²**.
This is the resale-relevant number — but note it lands *right on the Avoti-belt achieved-share
evidence* (Tērbatas 82a €1,637 ask, Bruņinieku €1,860 ask, building's own €1,826–1,891 realised),
which after their own asking→achieved haircut sit ~€1,500–1,750 achieved. **Convergent.**

**(d) Floor / view (small plus).** Flat 41 is **5/6** with SE+W aspect — top-ish, dual-aspect,
light. Most cheap east-Centre comps are low floors (Tērbatas 82a 1/5, Matīsa 101 1/5, Čaka 26 1/6).
A high, bright, dual-aspect floor in a pre-war building is a genuine premium over a ground/first
floor — call it **+5–8%**. (Caveat: 5/6 with a possible walk-up matters; lift status unconfirmed —
if no lift, the floor premium shrinks toward zero for some buyers.)

**(e) Avoti vs prestige (already in the base).** Handled by choosing the east-Centre base, not the
€3,000 benchmark. No further haircut — would double-count.

**(f) The furnishings (bundled — value the *used resale* of what conveys, NOT retail).** The flat
conveys substantially all furniture + premium appliances (Miele hood, Siemens cooktop, AEG oven,
custom joinery, electric sofa, designer sanitary). Retail-new this is plausibly €20–30k. But the
right valuation question is **"what would a buyer rationally pay to have exactly this already in
place"** — used, non-transferable taste, depreciating. Honest used/"already-here" value:
**~€8–14k**, midpoint **~€10–11k**. Custom-fitted joinery (kitchen, wardrobe) and good appliances
hold value best because removal+refit is costly; the sofa and décor depreciate hardest. This is a
**convenience premium, not an asset** — it does not appreciate and won't be recovered on resale
(sharpens the capped-upside point from prior work). Treat €10–11k as the slice of the price that is
*furniture, not real estate*.

## 4. Fair value for flat 41 (furnished share, Avoti)

**Real-estate slice (the share itself, unfurnished):**

- Comp-driven achieved value: **~€1,500/m²** (step c) × 58 m² ≈ **€87k**, +5–8% floor ≈ **€91–94k**.
- Cross-check against the building's **own realised** evidence: old renovated flats sold ~€1,826–1,835,
  ceiling €1,891 (attic). Flat 41 plausibly tops the *old-flat* field on renovation quality →
  ~€1,850–1,950/m² × 58 ≈ **€107–113k** as a realised-asking-equivalent for a genuinely
  top-of-building unit.
- These two methods **bracket** rather than agree: the external-comp/discount walk says ~€90k; the
  building's-own-sales walk says ~€107–113k. The gap is the asking-vs-achieved + "is flat 41 really
  top-of-building?" uncertainty. The building's own recent realised sales are the **stronger
  evidence** (same building, same share structure, actual closes), so weight toward the upper method
  but haircut it for "ask, not close": **real-estate slice ≈ €100–110k.**

**Add bundled furnishings (used/already-here):** **+€10–11k.**

**→ Fair value for flat 41 as a furnished share in Avoti: ~€110,000–121,000, midpoint ~€115,000.**
Confidence **medium** — the range width is honest (the share adjustment and asking-vs-achieved are
the soft joints).

## 5. Verdict — is €120k fair? Is €114k fair?

- **€120,000 (€2,073/m²):** **at-to-slightly-above the top of fair.** It sits ~10% above the
  building's own realised ceiling and above every Avoti-belt share comp. It is **defensible only on
  the "top-of-building, fully furnished" case** — i.e. you're paying €110k for the best real-estate
  unit in the building + €10–11k of conveyed furnishings. Not a steal; not a clear overpay either,
  *if* the renovation genuinely tops the building and the furniture conveys in writing. Without the
  furniture conveying, €120k for the share alone would be a mild overpay (~€9–13k above the
  real-estate fair slice).
- **€114,000 (€1,966/m²):** **squarely fair — right at the midpoint of the range.** This is the
  number the evidence supports best. As a live bid on the actual unit it's also the single strongest
  market signal in the whole file (an actual buyer, not an asking price), and it lands exactly where
  the comp walk does. If €114k is real, it is *not* a lowball — it's fair value, and it sets the
  realistic floor.
- **Where fair value actually sits:** **€110–121k, midpoint ~€115k**, *furnished*. The real-estate
  slice alone is ~€100–110k; the rest is furniture you won't recover.

**Reconciliation with the €1,890 ceiling and the €114k bid:** the building's realised ceiling
(~€1,891/m² = ~€109k for 58 m²) is the *real-estate* anchor; the €114k bid is ~€5k above it,
explained almost exactly by the conveyed furnishings. The two reconcile cleanly: **€114k ≈
ceiling-priced real estate + bundled furniture.** That coherence is reassuring — it means the
asking/bid range isn't detached from the building's own evidence.

## Bottom line for Niklavs

- **Fair value is ~€114–115k furnished** (~€105–110k of share + ~€10k of furniture you keep). The
  competing bid at €114k is, by this comp work, *fair* — not a lowball you can easily undercut.
- **What to pay:** anywhere **€114–117k is fair-to-good** for a furnished, top-of-building, high-floor
  unit. €120k is the **top of fair** — pay it only if (i) the furniture conveys in a **written
  itemised annex** (else you're at ~€109k of real estate for €120k), and (ii) you're confident the
  renovation genuinely tops the building (you've seen 41; you haven't seen the €1,890 comps' finish —
  prior research's correct humility).
- **Walk-away / negotiation:** the hard ceiling on the *real-estate* slice is the building's own
  ~€1,890/m² (~€109k). Above ~€121k all-in you're paying a premium the building's own sales don't
  support and that resale won't return (share + furniture both unrecoverable). Treat **>€121k as
  walk-away on price**; €120k as "fine, but get the furniture annex"; €114–117k as the comfortable buy.
- **The €114k bid changes the lever, not the value:** it firms the floor at fair value, so the room
  to negotiate *down* is gone — realistic outcome is €114–120k. The decision is no longer "is it
  overpriced" (it isn't, materially) — it reverts, as all prior work concluded, to the **values call**
  (accepting a share + capped upside for a flat he loves) and the life-fit.

## Honesty notes & gaps

- **Asking, not achieved.** 14 of 16 comps are *asking* prices; only the VZD/viszinis Centre
  aggregate (€2,185/m² achieved vs €2,431 asking, ~10% haircut) and the building's own folio sales
  are achieved. I applied the ~10% haircut explicitly in the walk, but per-comp achieved prices
  aren't knowable from portals. Shares plausibly cut *more* than 10% to close — so the real-estate
  fair slice could be a touch lower than stated. Directional.
- **Titled-vs-share is mostly assumed, not verified.** ss.lv/mm.lv cards don't show ownership
  structure. I flagged the two comps that *did* surface a share/communal signal (Tērbatas 82a,
  Bruņinieku 28 from prior work); the rest are assumed titled. If some "titled-assumed" comps are
  actually shares, the titled benchmark is overstated and flat 41 looks relatively *better* priced.
  The share discount in the walk leans on prior research's ~30%, not on a matched pair in this set —
  the single biggest soft joint.
- **Avoti-belt comp set is genuinely thin and heterogeneous.** Avotu 16 is a 1897 *2-storey
  wooden-frame* building (lower stock than #113's solid 6-storey brick) so its €1,200/m² understates
  #113; Tērbatas 82a is ground-floor + communal. No perfect "renovated brick 2-room share, high
  floor, Avoti" twin exists on the market right now — flat 41 is, fittingly, somewhat unusual. Said
  plainly: the €110–121k range is built from adjacent-not-identical comps + the building's own sales,
  not from a clean matched twin.
- **Floor premium assumes lift / acceptable walk-up.** 5/6 is a plus if there's a lift; a meaningful
  minus for some buyers if it's a 5-floor walk-up. Unconfirmed — would tighten the range.
- Photo/finish quality taken as given (buyer has seen it); not judged here.
