# Niklavs — what he's actually after isn't recognition, it's usefulness

**Observation (2026-06-01, Zezima session).** Niklavs, talking about burnout from a heavy stretch of building (the brain system, the shipping agent), first framed the pain as "no recognition — it gets forgotten under other burning topics." Pressed on whose recognition, he corrected himself: *"i dont know tbh, i think i just want it to be useful. I want this stuff that im doing here to not be just for show."*

**The thing under it.** Not validation-hunger. The fear is that effort-heavy, elaborate work is *theatre* — impressive-looking but not load-bearing. The absence of external use-signals (applause, reliance he can see) removed his usual proof-of-usefulness, so from the inside he can't tell show from useful, and the mind fills the gap with the worst answer.

**How to apply.** When he doubts a piece of his own work, the useful instrument is *reliance*, not recognition — "do I reach for this / would I miss it if it were gone," not "did anyone notice." Don't comfort the fear away; it's a good fear. Help him sort show from load-bearing ruthlessly. Connects to the brain's standing over-build pattern (minimal-action lens).
