## The three axes

### 1. Asset & price — PRO (P6, med-high confidence)
- €1,714/m² is **~10–16% below comparable asking** when framed correctly: against the
  Duntes Ozoli project itself (~€2,000/m² for 2-rooms) and the Pētersala/centre-adjacent
  segment (€2,033–2,478/m²). It only looks dear vs the raw Sarkandaugava district avg
  (~€1,071/m²) — the wrong comp set (old/small Soviet stock).
- The €100–160k 2-room band is the **most liquid** slice of the Riga market.
- Owned land + loggia + courtyard parking add value (Riga secured parking commonly +€5–15k).
- Rent optionality ~€470–550/mo (~4.5–5% gross yield) if his plans change.
- Riga market: stable, modest 2026 upside (Baltic laggard — less froth, less downside).
- **Resale watch-item:** the "cheap Sarkandaugava" postal reputation means a future buyer
  may anchor low and negotiate — but he's entering below market, which is the cushion.

### 2. The building — MOSTLY PRO, with one real unknown (P7, med confidence)
- 2006-completed (marketed 2007), ~50 m cascading tower, **3 sections (7/13/16 floors),
  217 apartments**, architect Ugrimovs, built by Budimex Latvia. Class **C** energy
  (73.1 kWh/m²/yr) — typical mid-2000s, better than Soviet stock, short of modern A/B.
- €0.95/m² management (~€67/mo) is **normal-to-slightly-high but justified** by 24h
  security + video + large grounds. Individual heat meters + regulators (verify present).
- **Flags:** (a) developer SPV is **liquidated** → no new-build warranty, treat as fully
  second-hand; (b) self-managed by a **dzīvokļu īpašnieku biedrība** — competence varies;
  (c) it won a 2008 **"Bietes balva" anti-prize** — *aesthetic/urban-fit only*, NOT a
  structural or build-quality criticism (no defects/litigation found); (d) listing says
  **brick**, aggregator says **monolithic RC** — for a 16-storey tower RC frame is far
  more plausible; confirm from the technical passport.
- **THE central question: the reserve fund (uzkrājuma fonds) + planned major repairs.**
  A 20-year building with many terraces, flat roofs, and first-gen lifts has foreseeable
  big-ticket items. Underfunded reserve → surprise special levies. This is obtainable
  only by document request from the biedrība, not the web.

### 3. Location & daily life — MIXED, profile-dependent (P5, med-high confidence)
- Sits on the **seam of Sarkandaugava / rising Skanste / Pētersala**. **NOT central** —
  listing files it "centrs" but Wikipedia + Latio file it Sarkandaugava/Northern; ~3 km,
  ~15–20 min transit to Old Town ("10 min by car" was the honest tell).
- **Pros:** Sky&More mall across the road (groceries/pharmacy/gym/dining), kindergarten in
  the building, ER hospital next door, hilltop views from upper floors, genuine Skanste
  "rising area" upside.
- **Cons:** public transport **lags the area's growth** (a Riga council member flagged it
  as urgent); busy through-road + mall traffic = noise (worse on low/street-facing floors —
  this unit is 9/16, helps); immediate Sarkandaugava/Pētersala surroundings still
  transitional/industrial in patches. Planned Skanste→Central Station tram is upside-if-
  delivered, multi-year, no firm date.
- **Best fit:** a car-owning owner-occupier who values the Sky&More convenience and the
  Skanste bet, on a higher/quieter floor. **Weak fit:** transit-dependent, wants a
  finished walk-to-centre neighbourhood today.

### 4. The money — manageable, concrete (P8, med-high confidence)
- **One-off to buy: ~€2,150–2,300** (1.5% ownership state fee ~€1,800 dominant; mortgage
  registration 0.1%; notary; valuation; agent normally seller-paid). Up to ~€3,700 if the
  bank charges a full 1.5% loan-issuance commission — shop that.
- **All-in to own: ~€730–840/mo** averaged over the year — ~€523/mo mortgage (€102k @
  ~4.6% over 30yr, 15% down) + ~€67 management + heating/utilities + ~€8 NĪN + insurance.
  Mortgage is ~65–70% of it; heating is the seasonal/building-specific swing.
- **Rate is an estimate, not a quote** (6M Euribor ~2.55% falling + ~1.5–2.2% margin).
  ±€30–50/mo per 0.5%. Get a written bank offer.
- **Altum young-specialist guarantee** (≤35 + qualifying education) → down payment as low
  as 5% (€6k), bigger loan + 2.4% fee. Family guarantee if a dependent child. Eligibility
  depends on his age/situation.
- **NĪN tip:** declare residence before Jan 1 → reduced Riga rate (~€80–115/yr vs ~€600
  at the 1.5% base). Tax is on cadastral value, not price.

## Pros / Cons at a glance

**Pros:** clean ownership; ~10–16% below comparable price; most-liquid market band; owned
land + parking + loggia; solid 2006 RC build in "very good condition"; serviced building
(security/video); rising-area (Skanste) upside; rent optionality ~5% yield; carry cost
likely comparable to or below central-Riga rent.

**Cons:** not central despite the listing label; transport lags the area now; through-road
+ mall noise; transitional surroundings; self-managed building + liquidated developer (no
warranty); **reserve-fund/major-repairs risk is unquantified**; "cheap district" resale
narrative to overcome; Class C (not A/B) energy → mid-range heating, not minimal.

## What to keep in mind / verify before committing (priority order)
1. **Reserve fund + planned major repairs.** Ask the biedrība in writing: reserve balance,
   monthly contribution, what €0.95/m² covers, list of upcoming works (roof/terraces/
   facade/lifts) and whether funded or levy-bound. Request 2–3 yrs of community meeting
   minutes. *This is the deal's biggest hidden variable.*
2. **Zemesgrāmata folio:** confirm true dzīvokļa īpašums, seller is sole owner, **no
   mortgage/seizure/encumbrance**, and the **land is owned (not leased / piespiedu noma)**.
3. **No arrears:** written confirmation from the manager of no management/utility/heating debt.
4. **Bank offer in writing** (margin, LTV, term, issuance fee, monthly) + Altum eligibility
   if ≤35. Confirm the valuation supports €120k.
5. **Cadastral value** from Kadastrs (drives NĪN). Declare residence before Jan 1.
6. **Actual heating/utility bills** — seller's last 12 months (Class C + many terraces).
7. **The specific unit:** floor 9/16 is good for noise/views; confirm orientation (street vs
   courtyard), and do a flood-map check (low-lying area, building marketed "on a hill").
8. **Confirm structure** (RC vs brick) from the technical passport; minor, but the listing
   is loose with facts (2006 vs 2007, "centrs" vs Sarkandaugava, brick vs RC) — verify all.

## Recommendation (provisional — flexes on his 3 framing answers)
**Lean BUY-WORTHY → proceed to due diligence.** Unlike the first apartment, nothing here is
structurally disqualifying; it's a fair-to-good deal on a sound asset. The gating checks are
the reserve fund (#1) and an honest location-fit call. How it flexes:
- **Rent now ≥ ~€700/mo** → the buy is financially compelling vs renting; lean yes.
- **Settling in for years** → location quirks matter less, Skanste upside accrues to him;
  lean yes. **Stepping-stone (sell in 2–3 yrs)** → the ~€2.2k buy cost + resale-narrative
  friction + flat Riga prices weaken it; be more cautious.
- **€120k is comfortable** → fine. **A stretch** → the reserve-fund levy risk and rate
  sensitivity matter more; keep a cash buffer, prefer 30-yr term, maybe Altum to preserve cash.
