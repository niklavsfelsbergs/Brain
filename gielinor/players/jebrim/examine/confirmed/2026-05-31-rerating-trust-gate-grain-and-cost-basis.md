# Trust-gate at the grain the bias and cost-basis actually live at — over-coarse gating produces false quarantines, not just false savings

**Observation ([[S132_32ff1025_shipping-savings-routing-optimization|S132]], 2026-05-31, project-close corrections).** My re-rating trust-gate ran at **engine grain** — one bias ratio per carrier engine. Niklavs landed two corrections showing that grain was too coarse in both directions:

1. **False negative from coarse grain.** maersk_eu aggregated to 0.50× and I quarantined the whole engine. Split by service lane, **MAERSKFR validated 1.00×** — accurate, a valid destination — while MAERSKUK/SE (~0.49×) were what dragged the aggregate down. The blanket quarantine buried a real **€249k/yr** lane (French UPS→Colis-Privé). A bias that lives at lane grain, read at engine grain, hides good lanes behind bad ones.
2. **Wrong cost basis read as engine bias.** The UK ~2× "gap" wasn't a broken engine at all — Picanova self-operates the injection truck (billed separately), so UK cards correctly price last-mile only. Comparing a last-mile-only card against a truck-inclusive actual *looks* like 2× engine bias and isn't.

**Rule for myself.** Before trusting OR quarantining a rate engine: (a) check whether the bias is uniform across its service lanes — if the ratio's IQR is wide or multi-modal, split to lane grain and validate each, don't quarantine the aggregate; (b) confirm the **cost basis matches** (door-to-door vs last-mile-only / self-injection) before reading a ratio as bias — a basis mismatch masquerades as engine error. The trust-gate's *grain* is itself a parameter; setting it too coarse manufactures false negatives (buried real lanes) exactly the way the [[2026-05-31-rerating-mirage-guard-capability-and-noise]] mirage manufactures false positives.
