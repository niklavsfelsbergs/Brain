# A switch recommendation must name the incumbent it exits, not just the winners

**Draft (2026-06-22, [[S285_2bf7cf70_orwo-tender-logic-walkthrough-and-us-disregard|S285]]).** Self-observation, ORWO tender.

**The moment.** I built a screenshot-ready ORWO routing summary for Niklavs to share with Andrea: a lane→carrier table headed "keep DHL on DE domestic; GB→Maersk; AT+CH→GLS; −€282k/yr." It named only the *winning* carrier per lane. Niklavs immediately asked **"wait we drop UPS completely?"** — because the table never said that the lanes moving to Maersk/GLS are **UPS** lanes today (GB 88%, AT 65%, CH 88% UPS, mart-verified). The recommendation *is* a UPS-from-EU exit, and I'd presented it as if it were only an addition.

**The pattern.** When I recommend a switch — carrier, vendor, tool, source — I default to foregrounding **what's adopted** and leave **what's displaced** implicit. But the displaced incumbent is exactly what the principal needs to judge the *non-rate* cost: the operational disruption, the relationship/leverage hit, the loss of a fallback. A "move GB→Maersk" line hides that it's "remove GB from UPS." The selection is half the decision; the displacement is the other half, and it carries the risk.

**How to apply.** Any switch/routing recommendation states the displacement explicitly alongside the selection — "this moves ~86k EU trks **off UPS**, leaving UPS only the US lane" — and surfaces the cost of exiting that incumbent (leverage, ops, fallback), not just the rate win. Especially in a deliverable the principal will forward: the reader needs to see who loses, not only who wins.
