# Pre-departure prep checklist — what to sort out while still employed (2026-06-09)

> For the RateProof venture (see [[2026-06-09-rateproof-business-sketch]]). Organizing principle: **sort first by what expires the day you leave.** Live invoice data, the running systems, carrier relationships, colleagues, and a sanctioned reference case all evaporate on the last day — do those with one foot still inside. The rest can wait.

## A. Things that expire when you leave — do these NOW

1. **Get the methodology into your *own head*, not just the system's.** The biggest risk the leverage hides. In a client meeting you can't spawn a sub-agent. Be able to explain and defend, cold: the full re-rating chain (capability matrix → rate engine → cost matrix → scenarios); **why the trust gate exists and why bias doesn't wash out in aggregate**; cost-basis rules, the 11-bucket decomposition, DIM/divisor mechanics, fuel/peak/demand surcharge structures per carrier; the quarantine-grain logic (service-lane, not engine). *Test:* can you whiteboard the whole tender method to a skeptical CFO in 20 minutes with no notes? Until yes, the system understands it and you don't — and that gap is invisible until a client exposes it.

2. **Capture the reference case cleanly and legitimately.** The ~€378k / ~13% number + the caught-sourcing-error story *are* your sales engine. While inside: pin the exact defensible figures; write the anonymized case study; line up a sanctioned reference/testimonial if the relationship allows; get clarity on what you're permitted to cite.

3. **Mine the live data for things only real invoices teach.** Irreplaceable: **invoice DQ patterns** (every way carrier billing goes wrong — pure gold, only learnable at volume); **carrier rate-card mechanics generalized** beyond the specific contracts seen; **negotiation dynamics** (what levers move carrier pricing) — learn from colleagues/the live tender while you can.

4. **Seed a clean-room benchmark understanding.** The industry's hardest moat is a proprietary rate-benchmark DB. You **cannot take the employer's negotiated rates** — but you *can* build your own understanding of market rate ranges from public tariffs + what you legitimately learn, so you start your own benchmark from day one instead of zero.

## B. Clean-room asset rebuilds — start now, but the *learning* needs live data

5. **SCM — generalize, simplify, correct.** The productizable version is carrier-agnostic and config-driven, not employer-wired: any shipper's data in, monitoring/alerting out. Rebuilt clean-room (own code). List the specific corrections now while you remember what's wrong with it.

6. **Separate the portable *framework* from the non-portable *data* in the tender engines.** Portable (yours to rebuild): the architecture — capability matrix, the `Surcharge` ABC, two-phase BASE→DEPENDENT, the re-rating trust gate, the report templates. NOT portable: the employer's negotiated rates, mart, invoice data, anything built on company time/tools. The framework is the IP you walk out with; the data stays.

7. **Write the method as your own playbook.** Turn the tacit process into a documented Tender SOP + Diagnostic SOP — *your* clean-room articulation. Doubles as the cure for #1: you don't understand it until you've written it down without looking.

## C. The gate that can void everything — resolve early

8. **The IP / legal boundary.** Read the employment contract for IP assignment, **non-compete, non-solicit, confidentiality.** Specifically: can the employer be a *first client*? Can you run a side business at all? Where's the line on knowledge-in-your-head vs their data? May need a lawyer. **Build nothing for the business on company time or company tools** — total separation. Get this clear before investing in B; the answer can reshape the plan.

## D. Anytime — lower urgency

9. Entity/legal structure, landing site, positioning copy, and — the real bottleneck — **starting to build a pipeline/network.** These don't depend on access, so they wait until A–C are moving.

## Priority order

**#8 (boundary) and #1 (own the method) first** — one can void the plan, the other is the gap the leverage actively hides. Then #2–#4 (capture what expires), then the clean-room rebuilds. Everything in section A is on a clock; section D isn't.
