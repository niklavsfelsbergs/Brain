# A competitor carrier-switch reprice must carry the competitor's OWN surcharge stack before it's a verdict

**Observed:** [[S283_60de5609_orwo-tender-assumptions-and-carrier-questions|S283]] (60de5609), 2026-06-22, ORWO tender. I carried the GLS "~-EUR509k/yr, verdict SAFE" headline from the prior session across this whole session as the answer - in the where-we-stand readout, the point-1 discussion, the dims analysis. Niklavs' "show me each assumption for each question" push made me read the GLS engine, and only then did I find it models NONE of GLS's own Energy (20.5%) + Dieselfloater (4.1%) + ClimateProtect (2.5%) surcharges - ~25% on base, which the EU-tender GLS engine had modeled. The comparison added the incumbent's surcharges to the current side but not the competitor's to the GLS side. The -EUR509k was a freight-base figure dressed as a full-cost verdict, and could flip the -EUR238k domestic driver.

**The pattern (verify-the-thing, surcharge-completeness arm):** for a CARRIER-SWITCH reprice (no own invoices to trust-gate), "the card parses to the cent" proves the BASE rate is right - it says nothing about whether the engine applies the carrier's floating surcharge stack (fuel/energy/diesel/toll), which is usually NOT on the offer card (it floats per GTC). A modeled competitor number that omits the competitor's fuel is structurally an UNDER-count, and an asymmetric comparison (incumbent full-cost vs competitor freight-only) systematically overstates the switch saving. The trust-gate I leaned on (0.9992 etc.) only validates own-cost engines; the competitor engines had no equivalent and I treated the freight-base output as if they did.

**How to apply:** before relaying any carrier-switch saving as a verdict, check the competitor engine applies the SAME cost layers as the side it's compared against - especially the fuel/energy surcharge, which lives off-card. If the competitor's surcharge stack isn't in the engine, the number is freight-base, not full-cost - say so, and don't let a prior session's "safe" framing ride. The cheap check is one read of the competitor `constants.py` for the surcharge constants, against the incumbent's. Sibling: the same session I relayed dwarf-supplied oversize thresholds (120/150/300cm) as fact until Niklavs flagged they aren't in the ORWO contract - same theme: a modeled/borrowed input surfaced as a verdict-grade fact without checking its provenance or completeness.
