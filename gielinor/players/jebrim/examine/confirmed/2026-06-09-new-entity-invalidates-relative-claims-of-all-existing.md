# Inserting a new entity into a comparison invalidates the relative claims of ALL existing entities

**Observation ([[S185_e9821cdf_eu-tender-report-signoff|S185]], e9821cdf, 2026-06-09).** The carrier_overview report had a new competitor (`dpd_pl_current`, 16 segment wins) inserted into the lane×carrier grid, but the per-carrier prose was regenerated only for the new entity — the *other* carriers' narratives stayed at their pre-insertion values. The sign-off dwarf caught 4 stale win-counts (fedex 9→7, dhl_paket 10→7, gls 5→2, guell 10→9). But re-deriving the **whole field** against the parquet found more: maersk's "wins the **most** segments" superlative was now false (dpd_pl_current's 16 > maersk's 14), gls's "within-10% (9)" was now 6, and fedex's two within-10% *members* had swapped (its former FR-Bulky win demoted to a near-miss; a different segment dropped out). The new entity didn't just add a row — it **cannibalized specific segments** from fedex/gls/dhl_paket/guell, rewriting their win-lists, superlatives, and per-segment stories.

**Rule.** When a new entity enters a ranking / comparison / competitive field, **every relative claim about the pre-existing entities becomes a hypothesis** — counts, superlatives ("the most", "#2"), "X wins segment S", within-N memberships, "only/never/always". Re-derive the **entire field** from ground truth, not just the new entity's own row, and not just the entities the dwarf flags. The blast radius of an insertion is the whole comparison, not the insertion point.

**Generalizes** the [[S180_4766eb11_dpd-current-report-refresh|S180]] [[derived-report-prose-drifts-from-data]] lesson with a sharper trigger: a *new participant* is a louder drift signal than a data refresh, because it shifts comparatives, not just absolutes.
