# Solo / boutique analytics consulting economics

As of: 2026-06-09

- Positioning consensus (Philip Morgan, Jonathan Stark): specialization beats generalism for independents - clarity, memorability, trust, faster expertise accumulation. Vertical (industry) vs horizontal (problem) specialization; "strategic beachhead" then expand.
- Three positioning models: niche specialist, fractional head-of-data (time-boxed exec accountability, 5-8 hrs/wk/client), and productized consulting (fixed-scope fixed-price published offer - converts "should I hire this person" into "should I buy this thing").
- Pricing benchmarks (order-of-magnitude, 2025-2026 sources): US data consultants $150-350/hr, niche $400-500/hr; Germany senior BI/analytics ~EUR 800-1,120/day, EU top end EUR 800-1,700/day; fractional CDO retainer $12k-25k/mo; analytics projects $25k-250k, retainers $5k-50k/mo (lower confidence).
- Stark's product ladder: offers priced ~10x apart per rung ($10 ebook -> $1k roadmap -> $10k workshop -> $100k retainer). A fixed-scope fixed-fee diagnostic/audit is the standard entry rung - revenue plus qualified-lead funnel into larger engagements.
- Value-based pricing sweet spot ~5-6x ROI to client (capture ~16-33% of identified value). Gainshare (% of verified savings) works only where savings are quantifiable and independently verifiable - auditable invoice/procurement data is the clean case; attribution, client underreporting, and client-dependency are the named risks.
- AI leverage thesis: AI removes the execution bottleneck for a one-person practice, but only on top of standardized delivery; "orchestrating, not using" is the skill. AI-era solo tiers (paywall-sourced, indicative): $750-3k micro-consulting, $1.5-3k productized audits, $15-30k/mo retainers.
- What stays defensible when execution commoditizes: deep domain judgment in a narrow niche, a proprietary accumulating method, and owning the outcome (fractional/gainshare). Generic "I'll run AI analysis" is what commoditizes.
- The bottleneck for technical independents is lead-gen and sales, not delivery: needs a standing pipeline routine, positioning-before-leads, authority content; feast-or-famine and hourly-generalist billing are the canonical failure modes.
