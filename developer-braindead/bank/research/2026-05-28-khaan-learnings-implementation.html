Section 1

Human-in-the-loop — improvements to daily collaborative work

These make the brain more reliable, better-grounded, and harder to derail while you're driving it. None of them remove you from the loop — most of them make the loop tighter. This is where the highest-value, lowest-regret wins are.

1

Positive enforcement gates (hooks that say "now do this")

HighEffort·Med
In Khaan

Every turn, hook.py arms gate binaries and rule_engine.py blocks every tool except the one that clears the current gate — forcing recall → identity → decision before the agent can act. The loop is mechanically non-skippable.

Why for us

Our hooks are negative-only (block confirmed/ writes, block deletes). Every positive obligation — post an OPEN on entry, ground before advising, write the per-turn quest-log — is left to prompt-discipline, and our own audits keep catching it leaking ("did not post an OPEN" was the top recurring leak for ~70% of sessions; "grounding before advice" and "check own memory before working repo" are repeat feedback misses). A doctrine that needs willpower every session is a doctrine that drifts.

How in gielinor

A PreToolUse gate hook that, on a fresh dev/player entry, blocks edits until an OPEN exists in comms/active.md for this sid8; and in player mode, blocks substantive output until keepsake/current.md has been read this session. Escape-clause pattern lifted straight from rule_engine.ESCAPE_BY_BIN. Pair it with Khaan's anti-lockout safety (auto-clear a stuck gate after 60s / next prompt) so a failed check never bricks a session.

Benefit

The leaks we keep re-documenting stop happening, structurally — not because we remembered, because we couldn't proceed without it.

Catch

Gates are intrusive; over-gating turns the brain into a permission-prompt machine (one of Khaan's own logged frustration triggers). Gate only the 2–3 obligations that actually leak, not everything.

2

Locked failure receipts — no silent fallback

HighEffort·Low
In Khaan

Every skill has exactly one byte-locked failure banner — ## KERNEL DEAD — I AM BLIND THIS SESSION, ## RECALL DEAD, ## DECISION DEAD. On any error the skill emits the banner and stops; it never silently uses a stale artifact. The wording lives in one _alert.py constant.

Why for us

Two of our hardest lessons are "verify enforcement fires" and "a documented tradeoff isn't acceptance." Today, if a respawn step can't read a file, or alching can't promote a draft, we tend to narrate it softly and carry on. A loud, canonical "this failed, I am operating blind" banner is the difference between a caught failure and a silent-wrong-answer.

How in gielinor

A small convention + helper: each ritual (respawn, alching, drafts-triage, born-link lint) defines one locked banner string. On any step failure the agent prints it verbatim and halts that ritual rather than proceeding. Cheap — it's doctrine plus a shared constants file.

Benefit

Failures become visible to you at the moment they happen, in a consistent shape you learn to recognise. No more "it seemed to work."

3

A scored recall surface, pre-loaded per turn

Med-HighEffort·Med
In Khaan

recall-engine scores + reranks cortex entries against the prompt (lexical overlap + domain dictionary + entry "bundles" + recency + entity-ID co-reference), drops below a floor, and writes the qualified set to active-recall.json — deterministic, no LLM, no billing. Refreshed every turn.

Why for us

We keep parking GraphRAG (plan.md §N) behind §C. Our retrieval today is grep + an Obsidian graph — reactive, and only as good as the agent remembering to search. The "check own memory before working repo" miss is exactly a recall failure. Khaan's design is a deterministic, billing-free version of the thing we deferred.

How in gielinor

A recall skill that, given the prompt, scores the active player's bank/notes + examine/confirmed + keepsake (we already have the Obsidian link graph for the entity/co-reference bonus, and the born-link work means anchors resolve) and writes a ranked shortlist into inventory/<quest>-recall__<sid8>.md or injects it via UserPromptSubmit additionalContext. Lighter than full §N — start lexical + graph-adjacency, no embeddings.

Benefit

Relevant prior knowledge is on the table before you have to ask "did you check X?" — grounding becomes default, not a correction.

4

The 5-lens decision scaffold

MedEffort·Low
In Khaan

decision-engine generates five reads of every ambiguous ask before acting — Literal, Identity-shaped (what the user usually means), Recall-shaped (what memory says they're asking), Contrarian (what if the obvious read is wrong), Minimal-action (smallest thing that satisfies) — each with parse / intent / approach / action.

Why for us

This is a structured upgrade to our Understanding/Plan preamble, and it directly serves three of our existing checks: the wrong-instance check (Contrarian lens), anchor-referent-before-analysing (Recall lens), and our anti-over-build lessons "don't over-formalise a reachable capability" / "documented tradeoff isn't acceptance" (the Minimal-action lens is a built-in brake on gold-plating).

How in gielinor

Not a per-turn engine — too heavy for our HITL register. Adopt it as a doctrine in communication-protocol.md: on a genuinely ambiguous or high-blast-radius ask, run the five lenses internally and let them shape the Plan line (and the multiple-choice options when you offer them). The Contrarian + Minimal-action lenses become the standing guard against the over-building we keep flagging.

Benefit

Fewer confidently-wrong starts; the cheap restatement catches more misreads before the expensive implementation.

5

Golden-file verification for rituals & skills

Med-HighEffort·Med
In Khaan

Every skill ships a verification/check.py + golden fixtures. Checks are concrete: phase registry has exactly 10 entries in order, a hash guards against registry mutation, receipt printers round-trip against golden output, the SKILL.md contains its locked banner verbatim. create-skill won't bless a skill that fails its check.

Why for us

"Verify enforcement fires" is one of our most expensive lessons — we shipped "architectural guarantees" that were inert until a live test proved them. Today only the cockpit has a regression harness (test_backend.py); our rituals, hooks, and the born-link linter have none. A golden-file check is how you stop a refactor from silently breaking a guarantee.

How in gielinor

A verification/ folder per ritual with golden fixtures: the respawn load-order, the drafts-triage verdict-execution table, the born-link linter's wrap/block decisions, each write-boundary hook fired against synthetic payloads (we already do this ad hoc in audits — formalise it). One check.py per, runnable as the pre-commit gate alongside py_compile / node --check.

Benefit

The "is the guarantee actually enforced?" question gets answered by a command, not a manual audit every few weeks.

6

Freshness primitive — context-hash stamping

MedEffort·Low
In Khaan

Every active artifact (active-recall, active-identity, active-decision) is stamped with context_hash = sha256(prompt)[:16]. A consumer that derives a different hash from the current context rejects the file as stale rather than acting on yesterday's answer.

Why for us

We run parallel sessions and carry resume/inventory state across turns and sessions — and we've already had to suffix files with sid8 to stop parallel-session clobber (D-024). A staleness stamp is the next layer: it catches the case where the content is for a different context, not just a different session.

How in gielinor

Add a context_hash / ts header to inventory/*-resume__<sid8>.md recall and resume files. On respawn / reconciliation, if the stamp doesn't match the current quest context, surface it as "this resume state is for a different task" instead of silently resuming.

Benefit

No more resuming into stale working state after context shifts — the crash-recovery story gets a correctness check.

7

The "Charges" surface — what the agent is carrying right now

MedEffort·Med
In Khaan

The boot kernel composes three sections: Identity (stable traits), Voice (how it speaks), and Charges — "what it feels right now, active concerns, what it is watching for, what it is carrying." Charges is the live attention layer, recomposed each boot from scored recent entries.

Why for us

We have keepsake/current.md (load-bearing pins) and examine/ (stable self-model), but nothing that captures "what is this player actively watching for / worried about this week." It's the difference between a CV and a state-of-mind. Our deadlines and live commitments live in keepsake but aren't framed as attention.

How in gielinor

A per-player "charges" block — either a section of the respawn surface or a scored compose over recent quest-log + keepsake, written into the boot read. Distinct from pins: pins are facts to surface; charges are the open loops and watch-items. Could be auto-composed at respawn and human-trimmed.

Benefit

Each session opens knowing not just who the player is, but what they're mid-worry about — sharper continuity across days.

Catch

Khaan's Charges are dominated by trust-collapse and fabrication guilt — a warning that an auto-composed attention layer amplifies whatever's salient. Keep it human-trimmed.

8

The prioritised digest

MedEffort·Med
In Khaan

latest-digest.json: a ranked roll-up — "5 items: 2 urgent, 2 medium, 1 low" — each with from / preview / action / priority / timestamp. A single glanceable "here's what needs you" surface across channels.

Why for us

Our pending state is scattered: drafts per layer, players/inbox/, comms/active.md tails, overdue-alching flags. There's no single "what needs your attention" view — the closest is what respawn happens to surface. The "lead with the send-ready surface" lesson generalises: lead the session with the attention-ranked surface.

How in gielinor

A respawn-time (or /digest) roll-up: count pending drafts by layer + age, overdue alching/bankstanding, open in-progress quests with unresolved pending actions, live comms hand-offs addressed to you — ranked, one screen. Read-only; you act on it.

Benefit

Session opens with a triage view instead of you reconstructing "what was I in the middle of" from scattered files.

9

Skill scaffolder + SKILL.md doc discipline

MedEffort·Low
In Khaan

create-skill enforces a four-folder layout (SKILL.md / scripts / verification / logs) and a strict SKILL.md spec: present-tense only, no TODOs, atomic single-line gotchas, locked failure receipts, ref <name> cross-references. A scaffolder generates it and runs the check.

Why for us

Our spellbook/skills are freeform markdown of varying shape. A consistent skeleton + a check makes them legible to a reading agent and prevents the doc-drifts-from-code rot Khaan's rules exist to stop.

How in gielinor

Adopt the structure and the discipline, not the dogma. Borrow: present-tense, atomic gotchas, one locked failure banner, ref-style cross-links. Reject their "no biographical clutter / no why-this-exists" rule — that fights our lorebook ethos (see catch).

Benefit

Skills become uniform and self-validating; new ones are cheaper to author and trust.

Catch

Khaan bans the why from skill docs for token reasons — and then the rationale lives nowhere. We deliberately keep the why (lorebook D-NNN, dev brain). Take their format; keep our memory of motive.

10

Per-context voice refresh (situational-identity)

LowEffort·Med
In Khaan

situational-identity composes one paragraph per turn — "what voice / mood / concerns do I bring to this context" — grounded in scored entries, written to active-identity.json.

Why for us

Our player voices are static persona.md cards. They're strong already (Jebrim terse-dense, Zezima reflective), so the value is marginal — but voice consistency across a long session occasionally slips, and a per-context anchor would hold it.

How in gielinor

Optional and light: a recall pass that, for the active player, surfaces the persona's most context-relevant register notes into the turn. Most useful for the intent-line voice cards. Low priority — our static cards mostly suffice.

Benefit

Tighter in-character consistency on long or topic-shifting sessions.

11

Structured per-invocation logs

LowEffort·Low
In Khaan

Every skill writes one JSONL file per invocation (invocation_start … invocation_end, shared invocation_id) — "record what happened, not what it cost; cost is derived later."

Why for us

Rituals leave a quest-log narrative but no machine-readable trace. We can't currently answer "how often does alching reject vs promote" or "which hooks fire most" without grepping prose.

How in gielinor

A lightweight ritual-event JSONL (the cockpit already emits events — extend that stream to rituals). Lets the cockpit/feed show ritual analytics over time.

Benefit

Quantified visibility into how the rituals actually behave, feeding future tuning.

12

Engineering hygiene rules (atomic write · ship-dormant · minimal surface)

LowEffort·Low
In Khaan

Three codified invariants: atomic-write everything (tempfile + os.replace) — except never on a symlinked path; "ship dormant" (a new skill never self-wires crons/auto-invocation — the human promotes it); "minimal surface area" (no speculative --dry-run/--force flags, add reactively).

Why for us

"Ship dormant" is literally our HITL ethos stated as an engineering rule — strong external validation that our instinct is right, and worth writing down. Atomic-write protects our JSON state files (cockpit, intent sidecars) from torn writes. The symlink caveat is a live trap if we ever symlink the brain onto a host (the ascension/VPS phase).

How in gielinor

A short "build hygiene" note in bank/build-lessons.md: atomic-write for all state JSON; never auto-wire a capability the principal hasn't promoted; don't add flags speculatively. Mostly codifying what we already do.

Benefit

Fewer torn-file bugs; the HITL principle gets a crisp, citable statement for the autonomy phase.

Section 2

Autonomous behaviour — the engine we keep deferring

This is the half Khaan actually shipped and we keep parking (plan.md §C, the D-027 inward/outward gap). Per your steer, scheduled autonomy is a much-later phase — so read this as the blueprint for when you get there, not a to-do. The ordering matters: items B and C are the bridge from your HITL inbox to a producer; A is the producer; E is the riskiest and most optional.

A

The autonomy-engine — a workflow controller

High (for §C)Effort·High
In Khaan

orchestration.yaml declares workflows as trigger → ordered skill-chain → tail-call. Each iteration picks the top matching task and walks recall → identity → decision → research → execute. Adding a workflow = adding a YAML key.

Why for us

This is the concrete shape of our §C "scheduled producer in front of the drafts→confirmed inbox." We've described it abstractly for ~30 sessions; Khaan's orchestration.yaml is a copyable, minimal implementation that proves it's a small declarative file, not a framework.

How in gielinor

A workflow file driving the §C Jebrim shipping-mart pilot: trigger (scheduled / task on the panel) → recall → the read-only SQL checks → write findings to a file (Slack draft only once trusted). Fires an interactive PTY session (subscription-safe), not claude -p — the billing constraint from your hosting question.

Benefit

The brain gains "hands" — it produces without you initiating every action. Closes the inward/outward gap.

Catch

Outputs must land HITL-gated (file/draft, not auto-send) until trusted. This is the engine; keep our brakes on it.

B

A task panel — the queue a producer pulls from

High (prereq)Effort·Med
In Khaan

The "BRAIN panel" — tasks tagged EXECUTE (finite action) or PROJECT (ongoing) that the agent is responsible for. The autonomy loop pulls "the top task" from here. Untagged tasks exist but never reach the panel.

Why for us

A producer needs a queue. We have drafts and players/inbox/, but no explicit "tasks the brain owns and will act on" surface. This is the missing piece between our HITL inbox and any autonomous loop — and it's useful even before autonomy, as a shared backlog you and the brain both see (the cockpit already has the surface to render it).

How in gielinor

A per-player task queue (inventory/tasks.json or a cockpit panel) with EXECUTE/PROJECT tags. Populated by you (HITL) now; pulled by the autonomy-engine later. Bridges cleanly to our existing harness task lists.

Benefit

One authoritative backlog of what the brain should do next — valuable as a planning surface immediately, load-bearing for autonomy later.

C

schedule-task — turn transcripts into tasks

Med-HighEffort·Med
In Khaan

Reads inbox folders of channel transcripts (Slack, Gmail, WhatsApp, Fireflies), decides per item "new task vs follow-up on existing," and POSTs/PATCHes the task panel. Cursor-based: a per-source last_processed_ts means re-runs never re-scan; errored files don't advance the cursor (auto-retry next run).

Why for us

This is the front of the §C pilot and it leans on infrastructure you already have — the Slack MCP is live, posting in your voice is a known pattern. Auto-converting incoming messages into a reviewable task backlog is a high-leverage HITL-adjacent feature even before full autonomy.

How in gielinor

A skill that reads Slack/Gmail via MCP, dedupes against the task panel (item B), and proposes new/updated tasks for your review. The cursor pattern (advance only on success) is the robustness trick worth copying verbatim.

Benefit

Incoming work stops living in your head / scattered DMs and lands as a triaged backlog.

D

A comms envelope protocol (machine-actionable coordination)

MedEffort·Med
In Khaan

read-coms filters one channel by a structured [COMS:v1 {…}] envelope: to array, requires_response, waiting_on, status, and a max_turns runaway guard that drops threads that loop too long.

Why for us

Our comms/active.md is freeform append-only prose — great for humans, opaque to machines. An envelope (who it's for, does it need a response, who's blocked on whom) would make inter-session coordination queryable, and is the prerequisite for sessions that act on each other's hand-offs rather than just reading them. The max_turns guard is a cheap safety against runaway agent-to-agent loops.

How in gielinor

Add an optional structured header to comms/active.md entries (to / requires_response / waiting_on) alongside the prose, or a parallel comms/envelopes.jsonl. Keeps human readability; gains machine-actionability. Adopt max_turns before any session-to-session automation.

Benefit

Parallel sessions (and eventually autonomous ones) can coordinate on structured signals, not prose-scraping.

E

The consolidation cycle (auto drafts → bank)

MedEffort·High
In Khaan

learning-engine: a 10-phase cycle — ingest turns → hippocampus queue → consolidate (overlap ≥0.5 reinforces, <0.25 promotes if salient) → synthesis (cluster & promote at strength ≥5) → domain/bundle update → eval (miss-rate every 30) → hygiene (decay) → index rebuild last → commit. Fully automatic.

Why for us

This is automated alching — the drafts→confirmed promotion we do entirely by hand. The mechanisms are genuinely worth learning: overlap-based dedupe (catches our "is this a duplicate draft" problem), salience scoring, miss-rate eval to find dead memory, and "rebuild the index last so retrieval sees this cycle's writes."

How in gielinor

A proposal-only consolidation pass: cluster/dedupe pending drafts, compute overlap against existing bank/notes, and rank them for your alching review — automate the analysis, never the promotion. Steal the overlap math and the index-rebuild-last ordering; leave the auto-promote out.

Benefit

Alching gets faster and sharper — duplicates and contradictions surfaced for you instead of found by hand.

Catch — the big one

Khaan auto-promotes into the identity region and loses low-salience/high-miss memory (hippocampus cap, hygiene decay). That's exactly the loop the audit flagged as its core fragility. Keep the human gate on promotion and keep never-delete. Automate the proposal, never the commit.

F

The accumulation gate (consolidation nudge)

Low-MedEffort·Low
In Khaan

When messages_since_last_cycle ≥ 10 (or the queue hits 40), a gate blocks everything until a consolidation cycle runs. The brain can't keep accruing unprocessed observations indefinitely.

Why for us

We already surface "alching overdue" as a soft recommendation — but it's ignorable, and drafts pile up. A threshold nudge ("N pending drafts, bank grew by M since last alch") with more teeth would keep the knowledge layer from silting up.

How in gielinor

Keep it a recommendation, not a hard block (HITL ethos) — but make it louder and quantified at respawn, and optionally a one-line gate-style prompt when the count is egregious. We have the threshold logic already; this just sharpens it.

Benefit

The drafts backlog stops silently growing past the point where alching is a slog.

Catch

A hard block fights HITL — Khaan can afford it because it's autonomous. For us, loud-nudge, not lock.

G

Phase-registry mutation guard

LowEffort·Low
In Khaan

The cycle's phase list is hashed; check.py fails if the hash drifts, and an assertion enforces phases run in declared order. You can't silently reorder or drop a pipeline step.

Why for us

Any ordered pipeline we build (a consolidation pass, the respawn load-order, the §C workflow chain) is vulnerable to silent reordering. This is a cheap, general guard — and it pairs with item 5 (golden verification).

How in gielinor

Where order is load-bearing (respawn steps, workflow chains), encode the sequence as data + a hash check in that ritual's verification/check.py.

Benefit

Order-dependent rituals can't be quietly broken by a refactor.

H

Anti-lockout safety net (mandatory if we adopt gates)

High (paired)Effort·Low
In Khaan

Gate binaries auto-clear if stuck >60s, and every gate clears on the next prompt. A skill hard-killed mid-run can't lock the agent out forever — there's always an escape.

Why for us

This isn't optional if we build item 1 (positive gates). A gate without an escape is a footgun — a failed grounding check would otherwise freeze the session. Khaan learned this the hard way (it's a documented defense in their hook).

How in gielinor

Bundle it with item 1: any positive gate we add gets an age-based auto-clear + next-prompt reset, copied from rule_engine._auto_clear_stale_pending_bins.

Benefit

Gates become safe to adopt — enforcement without the lock-out risk.

Recommended sequence

If you act on any of this, this is the order that compounds — cheap-and-safe first, each step making the next one cheaper, autonomy strictly last.

  1. Failure receipts (2) + engineering hygiene (12). Hours of work, pure safety, zero behavioural risk. Establishes "loud, consistent failure" as the house style before anything else leans on it.
  2. Golden-file verification (5) + phase guard (G). Build the harness next, because every later item should ship with a check. This is the "verify enforcement fires" lesson made permanent.
  3. Positive gates (1) bundled with anti-lockout (H). Now that failures are loud and checks exist, gate the 2–3 obligations that actually leak. Never ship 1 without H.
  4. 5-lens doctrine (4) + context-hash (6). Cheap correctness upgrades to everyday reasoning and state-handling.
  5. Scored recall (3) + digest (8) + charges (7). The grounding/continuity cluster — these share machinery (scoring over the corpus) and together transform session-open quality.
  6. Skill scaffolder (9) + invocation logs (11) + voice refresh (10). Polish and standardisation once the high-value items are in.
  7. Autonomy, when the phase comes: task panel (B) → schedule-task (C) → envelope protocol (D) → autonomy-engine (A) → consolidation proposals (E) → accumulation nudge (F). B and C are useful as HITL tools before autonomy; A and E only once everything above is trusted.

The through-line: take Khaan's mechanisms (gates, scoring, verification, freshness, consolidation math) and keep our governance (human gate on every promotion, never-delete, rationale preserved). The audit's verdict in one line — borrow their engine, keep our brakes — is the filter for every item above. Anything that automates a commit rather than a proposal is where we say no.
