# Verify an inherited data source against the contract before building on it

**Observed:** 2026-06-15 ([[S244_bb5d1f1a_na-quota-torsten-revenue-correction|S244]]/bb5d1f1a). I computed the NA shipping quota with revenue from `dw.sales_fact`, because that's what the prior session's `decompose_quota.py` (topic 46) used. I carried it forward without question across an entire session of analysis — May/Q1 quotas, the channel-mix bridge, the SCM reconciliation. The principal caught it flatly: *"what the hell bro, we have revenue in the shipping mart."* The mart carries `fact_shipments.net_revenue_eur`; recomputing on it reproduced the SCM dashboard exactly (US May 26.52% ≈ 26.5%), where my `dw.sales_fact` number (26.15%, also wrong scope) had not.

**The miss:** I grounded the *numerator* rigorously (the shipping-mart cost, via the shipping-agent, "don't reason about the mart from memory") but **inherited the *denominator* blindly from a prior script.** A denominator is as load-bearing as a numerator — and when a divergence appeared (my 26.15% vs SCM's 26.5%), I first reached for scope/lens explanations and only surfaced the revenue-source as "axis 3 I can't assume" — I should have suspected the inherited source *first*.

**Rule:** When a prior script/notebook supplies a key table or column — especially a join target or a ratio denominator — treat it as a **hypothesis to verify against the canonical contract**, not a given. For mart work this is just the existing precondition extended to the *whole* formula: the mart is source #1 for the denominator too, and it often carries the field natively (here, revenue) so you never need the foreign join the old script reached for. A figure that won't reconcile with the productized/authoritative number (SCM) is a denominator-definition smell before it's a scope/lens one.

**How to apply:** Before building on an inherited source, ask "does the contract carry this natively, and is this the source the authoritative consumer (SCM/finance) uses?" Sibling of [[2026-06-12-which-variant-anchor-to-most-recent-active]] and the revalidate-borrowed-constants reflex — a borrowed *source* across a session boundary is a hypothesis, same as a borrowed constant.
