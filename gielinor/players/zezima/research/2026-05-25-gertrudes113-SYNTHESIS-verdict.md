## Headline

The research **does not disqualify this flat** — and it cleared the worst fear (ownership is
exactly as described, no nasty surprise; it's a functioning co-ownership with formal management).
But it **removes the "and it's cheap" comfort** and surfaces **one live flag** (financing) plus
**one new, specific legal check** (the 6th-floor addition). The whole decision now rests on two
gates Niklavs must close before any emotional commitment.

## What the research CONFIRMED (the good)

- **Ownership form is exactly as stated — no surprise.** *Kopīpašums* / domājamās daļas,
  one Land-Register folio (nodalījums **Nr. 5262**). A management biedrība ("Ģertrūdes 113",
  reg. 40008344006, **registered 05.02.2025**) manages the kopīpašuma domājamās daļas. One folio =
  not split into units = co-ownership confirmed. (R1, high)
- **Location is a fair-to-good fit for the profile** (central pre-war character, below-core price,
  car-light living). Far-east end of Ģertrūdes, **Avoti** apkaime — *not* the prestige klusais
  centrs, but a gentrifying bohemian belt (design shops, cafés, Ziedoņdārzs park), **~15–20 min
  walk to Old Town**, Čaka transit artery a block west. Fine by day; residual edge after dark;
  exact block (noise/safety gradient) unpinned. (R2, med-high)
- **Price is fair *for a share*.** €2,069/m² is ~30% below the renovated-pre-war-Centrs *clean-title*
  benchmark (~€2,900–3,100/m²); Riga is in a 2026 up-leg (~7–10% forecast), not flat. (R3, high)

## What CHANGED (the sobering)

1. **The price is the share price — not an extra discount that pays you for the risk.** ~a third
   off clean title (25–45% band; one hard comp: Bruņinieku 28, a registered-share Centrs flat at
   €1,860/m²) is roughly *what a share costs*. €120k looks cheap vs a titled renovated-centre flat;
   it's fair-to-full **for a share**, at the **shallow** end of the discount band. So you are NOT
   being over-compensated for taking on share risk — you're paying the going rate for it.
   *(This corrects the S066-T3 "you win the discount on entry" read — the discount IS the risk
   price, not a gift.)* (R3, low-med)
2. **Financing is a live flag — the research contradicts the selling agent.** R3: banks generally
   **won't lend on multi-apartment shares**, and **Altum reaches only *pusmāja* (half-house)
   shares** — this is an **~81-unit** building, the opposite case; no evidence the lending pool
   widened in 2025–26. The agent ("mortgage shouldn't be a problem… probably Altum") is the
   **seller's** agent. **A signed bank offer for THIS share is the gate; Altum is likely NOT
   available here.** If no bank lends and he's not all-cash, this is [[S056_e5df54a2_apartment-domajamas-dalas-research|S056]] again.
3. **The "added 6th floor" is a legal check, not just a feature.** Real, recent (~2020–23), created
   mansard/duplex units. Per VZD, **adding floor area recalculates everyone's domājamās daļas**;
   per CL 1068 the addition required **unanimous co-owner consent**. Must verify: consent obtained,
   permit valid, **shares recalculated and registered**. A latent defect here would touch the
   validity of what he's buying. *Double-edged:* that the addition happened at all is mild evidence
   the co-ownership **can** act collectively. (R1, med + inference)

Plus: it's **~81 apartments, not ~70** (possibly two buildings on one parcel) — confirms conversion
to titled units is permanently off the table (already accepted), and resale stays cash-buyer/thin.
Building body is 1913 with **57% physical depreciation (2024)** — renovated flat over an aged shell.

## Decision frame — two gates

This is **no longer a "walk."** It's a **plausible eyes-open buy** *if both gates clear*:

- **Gate 1 — Financing in writing.** A signed offer from a real bank (or confirmed Altum
  eligibility, which the research says is unlikely for a multi-apartment share). Not the agent's
  word. **If this fails, stop.**
- **Gate 2 — The Zemesgrāmata folio (Nr. 5262) + BIS.** One authenticated pull answers almost
  everything that's left:
  - a **registered *lietošanas kārtība*** binding successors to his exclusive use of *this* flat
    (the hinge that neutralises occupancy risk + de-risks resale/finance);
  - the **true co-owner count** and each share;
  - whether the **6th-floor addition was recorded and shares recalculated** (+ BIS permit/consent);
  - **encumbrances** (hipotēkas, aizliegumi, reālnastas) on the property and the seller's share.

If both clear, the residual is a values call that's legitimately his: *pay fair-share-price for a
characterful central flat I love, accepting a permanent cash-only/thin resale and co-ownership
exposure that a functioning, formally-managed building makes mostly theoretical.* That's a
defensible yes. The location and character are genuinely on his side.
