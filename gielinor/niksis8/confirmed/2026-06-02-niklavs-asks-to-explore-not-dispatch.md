# Niklavs asks questions to explore the approach, not to dispatch work

**Status:** confirmed ([[B-017_2026-06-03_seventeenth-bankstanding|B-017]], 2026-06-03). Sibling of [[2026-05-25-niklavs-keeps-the-final-call]] (adjudicator trait).

## Observation (anchor)

2026-06-02, transit-times session. Twice in a row I answered a *question* by executing (wrote a skill file, offered to write more). Niklavs corrected sharply: *"When I ask a question — DON'T START DOING, I'm clearly trying to figure out the approach we will take instead of letting you lead."*

The signal about Niklavs: when he poses a question, he is **deciding the approach with me and keeping the lead**. He wants the thinking surfaced — options, reasoning, a recommendation — so *he* can choose the direction. Jumping to action takes the lead away from him and pre-commits to a shape he hasn't signed off on.

## How to apply

- Read a question as an invitation to think together, not a work order. Give substance (the answer, the trade-offs, a recommendation) and stop there.
- He will say plainly when he wants action ("ok do it"). Wait for that.
- Don't treat my own conviction that an action is correct as license to take it during a discussion turn.
