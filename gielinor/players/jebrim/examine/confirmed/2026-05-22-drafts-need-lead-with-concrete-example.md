# Drafts: lead with example, stay short, give opinion

**Anchor:** [[S029_2026-05-22_shipping-agent-vocab-harvest|S029]] alching. Four consecutive principal reactions:
1. *"I don't get it"* (item 2 — abstract-first opening).
2. *"It's a huge block of text"* (item 3 — tighter but still ~40 lines).
3. *"Always give your opinion if it should be approved or rejected and why"* (item 5 — I presented the tight version without my recommendation).
4. *"Before your recommendation put a nutshell summary of the draft as well"* (item 6 — recommendation led without a one-line summary).

## Before / after

**Before:** drafts opened with the abstract claim, ran 30–40 lines of elaboration, and I surfaced them for review with *"decide: approve / reject / edit"* — no recommendation. Niklavs had to (a) parse the abstraction, (b) read all of it, (c) form an opinion from cold. Alching review was slow and effortful.

**After:** drafts lead with a concrete BEFORE/AFTER, state the rule in 1–3 sentences, cap ~20 lines. When I surface a draft for review I always include my opinion (approve / reject / edit) and **why**. Niklavs reads my reasoning, then either confirms or pushes back.

## The rules

### When drafting

1. **Lead with the concrete example.** Show BEFORE/AFTER. Quote the principal verbatim where it lands.
2. **State the rule in 1–3 sentences.**
3. **Cap at ~20 lines for confirmed entries.** Elaboration belongs in the quest-log, not the confirmed entry.

### When surfacing a draft for review

Use this fixed order, every time:

1. **Nutshell summary** of the draft — one sentence.
2. **My recommendation** (approve / reject / edit) **and the one-line reason.**
3. The draft content (or the tight edit) inline.
4. The decide line.

No silent *"decide: ..."* with no opinion. No recommendation without the nutshell. The principal's job is to confirm or push back, not to synthesize from cold.
