# Maersk ROW lane (FedEx Economy via FedEx PL) - public surcharge schedule mechanics

As of: 2026-05-27

- Maersk's ROW lane construction: Maersk contracted base rates + FedEx Poland's PUBLIC published surcharge definitions/percentages. The replay therefore needs FedEx PL public values, not Maersk numbers - except where Maersk explicitly overrides (non-standard-parcel RATES come from Maersk; only the FedEx definitions carry over).
- Governing fuel index: FedEx INTERNATIONAL FSC (USGC kerosene jet-fuel spot, EIA, ~2-week lag, % of transport charges), monthly -> weekly from 11 May 2026. Caveat: if the lane is actually Regional Economy the index flips to EU diesel - confirm the exact FedEx service name with Maersk.
- Headline discrepancy: Maersk's "49.50% published / 24.75% net after 50% discount" fuel figure does NOT match any sourced FedEx-published International FSC (export ~36.5-38.5%, import ~41-43% around $4/gal in early-mid 2026). Treat 24.75% as a Maersk-negotiated rate unless Maersk cites the exact public table row. (Provisional at research time: the 2026-06-03 follow-up later anchored Intl at 49.25% at $4.113 in June - the published table does climb with fuel, so re-check before leaning on this.)
- Structural step 11 May 2026: +2pp on international export FSC, +2.5pp on import (e.g. $4/gal export 36.5% -> 38.5%).
- Europe demand surcharge (the relevant one for an ex-PL Economy lane, NOT the US Oct27-Jan18 window): per-kg bands - 20 Oct-21 Dec 2025 Economy 0.10 -> 0.15 EUR/kg (Priority 0.30 -> 0.35); 22 Dec 2025-15 Feb 2026 steps back down.
- Non-standard parcel definition (FedEx PL, confirmed): standard = <=70kg actual, longest edge <=150cm, no other edge >70cm, sum of two longest sides <180cm; also non-standard if shifted centre of gravity, non-cuboid/irregular, longest side not the base, joined parts, or not machine-sortable. AHS-Weight triggers >25kg. Volumetric divisor 5000.
- ODA/OPA extended-area surcharges exist in the FedEx PL VASS schedule; exact PLN values were not publicly sourced (VASS PDF gated) and Maersk's treatment of them is unstated - open item.
