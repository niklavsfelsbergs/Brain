# Evaluating a Riga apartment buy — the axes and the hidden risks

**Claim.** Once ownership is clean, a home-buy decision splits into three orthogonal
questions, and the listing's headline numbers flatter on at least two of them.

**The three axes.**
1. **Good asset?** — price vs the *right* comp set, liquidity, building soundness.
2. **Good home for the life?** — location/daily-life fit (the part research can't measure).
3. **Carryable comfortably?** — true monthly cost, not "can the bank approve."

**Hidden risks / things the listing won't tell you (check before committing):**
- **Reserve fund (uzkrājuma fonds).** A building with no fund + a liquidated developer +
  self-management = future major repairs (facade, roof/terrace membranes, lifts on a 20yr
  tower) land as surprise **special levies** or get deferred. *This is the biggest hidden
  liability.* Ask the biedrība in writing: balance, contribution rate, planned works.
- **Usable vs headline area.** "70 m²" often includes the loggia (counted full) + the
  *dzīvojamā platība* (rooms-only) figure can be ~58. Recompute €/m² on real space — it can
  flip "below market" into "at market."
- **Right comp set.** A quality unit in a cheap postal district (e.g. a 2007 project in
  Sarkandaugava) must be compared to its project/segment, not the district average.
- **Price-per-m² framing, location label** ("centrs" when it's really Sarkandaugava),
  **build material**, and **build year** are all things listings state loosely — verify all.

**Money rules of thumb (Riga, 2026).** Buy one-off ≈ €2.1–2.3k on €120k (1.5% state fee
dominant; agent normally seller-paid). All-in to own a €120k flat ≈ €730–840/mo. Mortgage =
6M Euribor (~2.55%, falling) + ~1.5–2.2% margin; get a written offer. Altum young-specialist
(≤35 + education) → 5% down. **Declare residence before Jan 1** for reduced Riga NĪN.

**Negotiation levers that work:** an absent reserve fund (you inherit uncapped repair risk)
and an inflated headline area are both legitimate, hard-to-argue grounds for a price cut.
