# Lead with the send-ready surface for dispatch-type deliverables

**Observation ([[S078_1558d072_dpd-pl-round1-dispatch|S078]], 2026-05-26).** Building the DPD PL carrier dispatch, I produced the fully-annotated `questions_for_carrier.md` (each question wrapped in Why-we-need-this / Plan-on-response / priority tag) and treated *that* as the deliverable. The principal then had to ask twice for what he actually needed to act on: first *"I need just the questions to send out. Like this I did for fedex"* (he pasted the plain FedEx send-format as the template), then *"put them at the top of the dpd pl file so i can normally copy them."*

**Pattern.** For a deliverable whose endpoint is the principal **sending or pasting it externally** (a carrier dispatch, a Slack/email message, an export), the thing he acts on is the **clean copy-ready surface** — plain prose, no internal annotation. The annotated version is the right *internal* artifact (audit trail, cascade input) but it's not the deliverable; it's the working notes behind it.

**Rule.** When the work product is something to be sent/used as-is, lead with the copy-ready form (or place it at the top of the file), and keep the internal annotation below the fold. Don't hand over the annotated working version and treat the send-form as an afterthought.

Sibling to [[2026-05-26-check-own-bank-for-prepared-content]] and the global "clarify deliverable form" lesson — that one is about the output *format* (md/HTML/PDF); this is about the *annotation level / copy-readiness* within a format. There was a known send-format precedent (FedEx) I should have matched without being asked.
