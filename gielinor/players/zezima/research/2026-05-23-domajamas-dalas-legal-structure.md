# Domājamā daļa (ideal share of kopīpašums) — buyer/occupant's legal position under Civillikums

**Question.** Under Latvian Civil Law (Civillikums), what does owning a *domājamā daļa*
(notional/ideal share of a co-owned property, *kopīpašums*) mean for someone who wants to
legally live in one specific apartment of the building — as opposed to owning a registered
separate apartment unit (*dzīvokļa īpašums*)? Focus on the buyer-occupant's position:
(1) the use/occupancy arrangement (*lietošanas kārtība*) and whether it binds future
co-owners/successors; (2) the right of any co-owner to demand division of the co-ownership
at any time; (3) pre-emption / right of first refusal (*pirmpirkuma tiesības*).

**Date of research.** 2026-05-23.

**Confidence.** High on the statutory structure (articles quoted from the official
consolidated Civillikums and the official state legal portal LV portāls). Medium on two
fine points: (a) the precise legal weight of an *unregistered* usage arrangement against a
new buyer, and (b) the exact current wording/numbering of the 2022 reform provisions (the
5-year bar and the new "misbehaving co-owner" mechanism) as consolidated — sources agree on
substance but I quote the reform from explainers, not a verbatim consolidated read of the
amended article.

---

## TL;DR for the buyer-occupant

Buying a *domājamā daļa* and buying a *dzīvokļa īpašums* are **two different legal things**.
A *dzīvokļa īpašums* is a legally separated, standalone immovable (the apartment + its share
of common parts) registered as its own unit — you own *the apartment*. A *domājamā daļa* is
an **abstract fraction of the whole building** (e.g. 1/8) — you do **not** own any specific
apartment, even one you paid to live in. Your right to occupy "your" flat exists only through
a *lietošanas kārtība* (usage arrangement) layered on top of the co-ownership, and it sits
under a permanent statutory threat: any co-owner can demand the whole co-ownership be divided.

The three load-bearing risks, in order of how much they should worry an occupant:

1. **The occupancy right is contractual unless registered** (CL 1070-context). Registered in
   the Land Register, it binds successors/new buyers. Unregistered, its survival against a
   *new* buyer of another share is shaky — see §1 for the nuance.
2. **Any co-owner can force division at (essentially) any time** (CL 1074). Since 2022 there
   is a 5-year cooling-off bar, but with broad exceptions, and after 5 years the right is
   open-ended. Division can mean physical split, **auction sale of the whole building**, or
   buyout — the occupant can be forced out with compensation, not kept in place.
3. **Pre-emption right of other co-owners** (CL 1073) constrains *selling* a share to an
   outsider (2-month first-refusal window) — relevant to both buying in and exiting.

## Background: what *domājamā daļa* / *kopīpašums* actually is

**CL 1067** — definition (verbatim, official consolidated Civillikums via likumi.lv):

> *"Īpašuma tiesība, kas pieder uz vienu un to pašu nedalītu lietu vairākām personām nevis
> reālās, bet tikai domājamās daļās, tā ka sadalīts vienīgi tiesību saturs, ir kopīpašuma
> tiesība."*
>
> ("The right of ownership belonging to several persons over one and the same **undivided**
> thing, **not in real but only in notional (ideal) shares**, such that only the *content* of
> the rights is divided, is the right of common ownership.")
> — source: likumi.lv consolidated CL (Trešā daļa, Lietu tiesības).

So the share is **not a physical part of the building**. The arithmetic share (1/2, 1/3, 1/8)
does *not* correspond to a specific apartment or square-meterage. LV portāls states this
directly: a *domājamā daļa* is "something intangible" and "does not denote a precise physical
part of the property" — it is a percentage of the whole
(source: lvportals.lv, *Kas ir kopīpašums…* search summary; *Kas jāzina, iegādājoties daļas
kopīpašumā* 2021).

**CL 1072** — *"Katra kopīpašnieka domājamā daļa kopējā lietā pieder vienīgi viņam."* (Each
co-owner's notional share in the common thing belongs solely to him.) — i.e. you can sell/
mortgage/bequeath *your share*, but the share is the abstract fraction, not the flat.

**CL 1068** — disposal of the **common thing as a whole or in specific parts** requires the
consent of **all** co-owners (verbatim, via lvportals.lv 2025 explainer and Constitutional
Court case ref 238562):

> *"rīkoties ar kopīpašuma priekšmetu kā visumā, tā arī noteiktās atsevišķās daļās drīkst tikai
> ar visu kopīpašnieku piekrišanu."*

This is the structural pain point: physical/legal acts on the building (renovations beyond
maintenance, encumbering the whole, etc.) need unanimity. (CL 1068 has been to the
Constitutional Court — case id 238562, "Par Civillikuma 1068.panta pirmās daļas atbilstību
… Satversmes 105.pantam" — i.e. tested against the constitutional property-right guarantee;
it survived. *Inferred relevance: the unanimity rule is a known, litigated friction point.*)

**Contrast — *dzīvokļa īpašums*.** Governed by the separate *Dzīvokļa īpašuma likums*
(likumi.lv id 221382), a *dzīvokļa īpašums* is "a legally separated standalone immovable in a
residential building, consisting as a *lietu kopība* (aggregate of things) of the separate
property [the flat] **and** a corresponding notional share of the common property"
(lvportals.lv summary). Key practical difference: with a *dzīvokļa īpašums* you own the flat
as its own Land-Register unit — no co-owner can force you out via a division action against
the building, and you do not need a *lietošanas kārtība* to have the legal right to occupy it.

## 1. The use/occupancy arrangement (*lietošanas kārtība*) — and does it bind successors?

**What it is.** Co-owners can agree on a *lietošanas kārtība* — a usage arrangement that
allocates **who uses which physical part** of the common property (e.g. co-owner A uses
apartment 1, co-owner B uses apartment 2). This is the *only* mechanism by which a
*domājamā daļa* holder gets the right to occupy one specific flat. Without it, a co-owner has
only proportional rights to the whole, **not to any particular space**:

> "A buyer acquiring a *domājamā daļa* does **not** automatically gain rights to occupy a
> specific apartment. … Without a usage arrangement, the co-owner has only proportional rights
> to the property as a whole, not to particular spaces."
> — source: lvportals.lv, *Kas jāzina, iegādājoties daļas kopīpašumā* (2021).

**Legal nature.** The usage arrangement is treated as a **restriction on the co-owners'
property rights** (*īpašuma tiesību aprobežojums*), and as such it **can be registered in the
Land Register (zemesgrāmata)** (lvportals.lv, *Kopīpašums un domājamās daļas* 2017).

**Does it bind successors / a new buyer? — the crux.**

- **Registered → yes, it binds.** When the arrangement is recorded as a property-right
  restriction, subsequent co-owners and purchasers of a share must respect it. The 2017
  LV portāls explainer states this almost verbatim:

  > *"citu kopīpašnieku pienākums ir netraucēt šo lietojumu, rēķināties ar to arī gadījumā, ja
  > viņu vietā vai blakus viņiem par kopīpašnieku kļūst cita persona."*
  >
  > ("the other co-owners' duty is not to interfere with this use, and **to reckon with it even
  > in the case where another person becomes a co-owner in their place or alongside them**.")
  > — source: lvportals.lv, *Kopīpašums un domājamās daļas* (2017).

  This is the load-bearing sentence for the buyer's security: a **registered** *lietošanas
  kārtība* survives when another co-owner sells their share. The new buyer of the *other*
  share takes subject to your right to occupy "your" apartment.

- **Unregistered → weaker; survival against a new buyer is doubtful.** LV portāls' 2025
  explainer uses *"ieteicams … nostiprinot to zemesgrāmatā"* ("**advisable** … by securing it
  in the Land Register"), i.e. registration is *recommended* for enforceability rather than a
  strict precondition of the agreement's validity *between the signatories*. But an
  unregistered arrangement is fundamentally a **contract** between the people who signed it.
  *Inferred from CL general principles + the registration emphasis: an unregistered usage
  arrangement binds only the current contracting co-owners; against a new third-party buyer
  who did not sign and had no Land-Register notice, its binding effect is at best uncertain and
  likely unenforceable.* This is exactly why every source pushes registration. **For an
  occupant, this is the single most important practical safeguard: insist the lietošanas
  kārtība is registered in the zemesgrāmata, not merely a side contract.**

**If co-owners can't agree, a court can set the arrangement.** Where voluntary agreement is
impossible, *"to var noteikt ar tiesas spriedumu, vēršoties ar atbilstošu prasību tiesā"* (it
can be established by court judgment via an appropriate claim)
— source: lvportals.lv 2025, *Kopīpašuma lietošanas ierobežojumi un tā dalīta lietošana*.

> **Article-number caution.** Sources attach the usage arrangement to different anchors —
> commonly framed under the CL 1067-1075 *kopīpašums* block, and the disposal-consent rule is
> CL 1068. The verbatim CL 1070 on the official page reads *"Kopējās lietas dalīta lietošana
> pielaižama tikai tad, kad šo lietu var dalīt, bet arī šajā gadījumā lietošana samērojama ar
> atsevišķo daļu lielumu"* (divided use of the common thing is permissible only when the thing
> *can* be divided, and even then use is measured against the size of the separate shares). So
> the statutory hook for "divided use" is in this 1067-1075 cluster; the *binding-on-successors*
> doctrine is articulated in case law / the official portal rather than as one tidy article
> sentence. *(Treat the binding-on-registered-arrangement point as well-established doctrine
> rather than a single quotable article clause.)*

## 2. The right to terminate/divide the co-ownership (*kopīpašuma izbeigšana / dalīšana*)

**Any co-owner can demand division at (essentially) any time. CL 1074** (verbatim):

> *"Nevienu kopīpašnieku nevar piespiest palikt kopīpašumā … katrs kopīpašnieks var katrā
> laikā prasīt dalīšanu."*
>
> ("No co-owner can be forced to remain in common ownership … **each co-owner may at any time
> demand division**.") — source: likumi.lv consolidated CL; quoted identically by lvportals.lv.

The Supreme Court (Augstākā tiesa) treats the right to demand division as a **co-owner's
property right (*lietu tiesība*) that another co-owner cannot block**: a court "cannot reject a
co-owner's claim for real division brought under CL 1075, because it is a property right
established by CL 1074" (source: at.gov.lv case-law classifier 1067-1129; *Jurista Vārds*
case-law digest 242461). **This is the buyer-occupant's biggest structural risk:** you can pay
to live in a flat, register a usage arrangement, and still have a *different* co-owner
unilaterally start a division action that unwinds the whole co-ownership.

**Forms of division. CL 1075** — first the co-owners try to agree; failing that, the **court**
decides and may (source: lvportals.lv 2022 reform explainer + likumi.lv CL 1075 verbatim
*"…tiesa … piespriež katram reālas daļas…"*):

- **Physical/real division** (*reāla sadalīšana*) — award each co-owner a real part, with
  servitudes if needed. (Often impossible for an ordinary apartment building.)
- **Assign the whole (or a part) to one/more co-owners with monetary compensation** (buyout) to
  the others.
- **Sale by auction (*izsole*)**, proceeds split per shares. **This is the outcome that can
  force an occupant out of the building entirely.**
- Decision by lot in some allocation scenarios.
- **(2022 reform) Division of a residential building into *dzīvokļu īpašumi*** (apartment
  properties) — now the **primary** method a court uses when co-owners of a residential
  building dispute division. This is the "good" outcome for an occupant: the building is
  converted into proper apartment units and you end up owning your flat as a *dzīvokļa
  īpašums*. *(But it is the court's choice among methods, not the occupant's entitlement.)*

**2022 reform — the 5-year cooling-off bar.** The Civillikums was amended (the reform package
LV portāls dates to **entry around 1 May**, 2022 cycle) to add a restriction in **CL 1074
part 2**: a co-owner who acquired their share by transaction or court order **cannot demand
division earlier than five years** from registration of the ownership in the Land Register
**unless there is a *svarīgs iemesls* (special/important grounds)** — analogised to the CL 728
five-year bar on heirs' partition (source: lvportals.lv 2022, *Jauni tiesiskie mehānismi
kopīpašuma dalīšanas gadījumā*). Recognised "special grounds" examples include:

- a co-owner is **denied use** of their co-ownership rights by the others;
- fair, objective use/division proposals are **completely ignored** by other co-owners;
- other co-owners **fail their CL maintenance duties**, dumping the burden on this co-owner;
- the acquirer's intent was **genuine use, not speculation**.

> What the 5-year bar means for the occupant: it gives **medium-term stability** (a freshly-
> bought-in co-owner generally can't be force-divided out within 5 years), **but** the
> exceptions are broad and discretionary, and **after 5 years the open-ended CL 1074 right
> returns**. It dampens, not removes, the division risk.

**2022 reform — removing a "misbehaving" co-owner (new mechanism, ~CL 1074.1).** The reform
also added a route to **sell out a co-owner who breaches duties**: first a **closed auction
among the co-owners** (*slēgtā izsole kopīpašnieku starpā*), with an **open auction** as
fallback if no co-owner buys the share (source: lvportals.lv 2022). *(Article number 1074.1
is from the explainer; treat the exact numbering as medium-confidence.)*

## 3. Pre-emption / right of first refusal (*pirmpirkuma tiesības*)

**CL 1073** governs it. When a co-owner of immovable property **sells** their *domājamā daļa*
to **a person who is not already a co-owner**, the other co-owners have a **statutory
right of first refusal for two months** from receiving a copy (*noraksts*) of the purchase
agreement (source: likumi.lv CL 1073; lvportals.lv 2025 *Kopīpašnieka pirmpirkuma tiesības*):

> *"Ja kāds nekustama īpašuma kopīpašnieks atsavina savu daļu [personai, kura nav
> kopīpašnieks], tad pārējiem kopīpašniekiem divu mēnešu laikā [skaitot no pirkuma līguma
> noraksta saņemšanas] pieder pirmpirkuma tiesība."*

How it constrains buyer/seller of a share:

- **Scope — outsiders only.** The right fires **only when the share is sold to a non-co-owner**.
  *"Likumiskā pirmpirkuma tiesība nav izlietojama, ja domājamā daļa tiek pārdota citam
  kopīpašniekam"* — it does **not** apply to a sale to an **existing co-owner**; in that case
  the seller may freely pick which co-owner to sell to (source: lvportals.lv 2023, *Kopīpašuma
  domājamās daļas pārdošana pārējiem kopīpašniekiem*).
- **Gifts are excluded.** Pre-emption (and redemption) **cannot** be used when the share is
  **gifted** (*dāvināts*) rather than sold (source: lvportals.lv pre-emption explainers).
- **Mechanism.** A co-owner exercises the right by **stepping into the buyer's position on the
  same terms** (matching price/conditions).
- **Violation → redemption right (*izpirkuma tiesība*, CL 1381 et seq.).** If the seller's
  fault prevented the co-owners from exercising pre-emption (e.g. they weren't notified), the
  co-owners may invoke the **redemption right** to reclaim the share from the third-party buyer
  under CL 1381 ff. (source: lvportals.lv 2025 *Kopīpašnieka pirmpirkuma tiesības*; *Pirmpirkuma
  tiesības un to neievērošanas tiesiskās sekas* 2024).
- **Forced-sale auctions.** Whether co-owners have pre-emption **at a forced auction** is a
  litigated point with a dedicated 2024 LV portāls e-consultation (*Vai piespiedu izsolē
  kopīpašniekam ir pirmpirkuma tiesības*) — *flagged as a separate, nuanced question; not fully
  resolved here.*

**Practical read for the buyer.** Pirmpirkuma tiesības is mostly an **exit/entry friction**
rather than an occupancy threat: when you (later) want to **sell** your share to an outsider,
you must first offer it to the other co-owners and wait out the 2-month window; and when you
*buy in*, the seller must have cleared the other co-owners' pre-emption. It doesn't protect or
threaten your right to *live* there — that's §1 (usage arrangement) and §2 (division) territory.

## How the three pieces combine — the buyer-occupant's net position

A *domājamā daļa* buyer who wants to live in one flat is, legally:

1. an owner of an **abstract fraction of the whole building**, not of the flat (CL 1067, 1072);
2. whose **right to occupy a specific flat** exists only via a *lietošanas kārtība* that should
   be **registered in the zemesgrāmata** to bind future buyers/successors (registered → binds,
   per the 2017 LV portāls doctrine; unregistered → likely only binds current co-owners);
3. exposed to **CL 1074** — any co-owner can demand division (after a 2022 5-year bar with broad
   exceptions), with outcomes ranging from conversion into proper *dzīvokļu īpašumi* (good) to
   **auction sale of the building and a forced buyout** (occupant loses the home, gets money);
4. and bound by **CL 1073** pre-emption mechanics on any sale of a share to an outsider.

Compared with a **dzīvokļa īpašums**, the *domājamā daļa* occupant trades durable, unit-level
ownership for a fractional interest whose occupancy security is only as strong as a registered
usage arrangement and is permanently exposed to another co-owner's division right.

## Gaps & open questions

- **Unregistered usage arrangement vs a new third-party buyer.** Sources strongly imply a
  *registered* arrangement binds successors and an *unregistered* one is contractual (binds only
  signatories), but I did not find a single statute sentence or cited judgment squarely deciding
  that an unregistered *lietošanas kārtība* is unenforceable against a new buyer. *(Medium
  confidence; would confirm with the Kudeikina dissertation or a specific Supreme Court ruling.)*
- **Exact consolidated wording/numbering of the 2022 reform.** The 5-year bar (CL 1074 part 2),
  the *dzīvokļu īpašumi* division method, and the misbehaving-co-owner mechanism (CL 1074.1) are
  taken from the LV portāls reform explainer, not from a verbatim read of the *currently
  consolidated* amended articles. Numbering and precise thresholds should be re-verified against
  likumi.lv before relying on them in a transaction.
- **Pre-emption at a forced (execution) auction.** There is a dedicated 2024 LV portāls
  e-consultation indicating this is contested; not resolved in this writeup.
- **Effective date precision of the 2022 reform.** LV portāls phrasing pointed to a ~1 May entry
  in the 2022 cycle; the exact in-force date was not pinned to the consolidated act here.
- **Out of scope by brief (other penguins):** bank/mortgage financing of share purchases;
  resale value and the practical process of converting a *domājamā daļa* into a *dzīvokļa
  īpašums*; operational maintenance-cost and co-owner-dispute dynamics. Touched only where the
  law required (e.g. CL 1071 cost-sharing by share, the *dzīvokļu īpašumi* division route).
