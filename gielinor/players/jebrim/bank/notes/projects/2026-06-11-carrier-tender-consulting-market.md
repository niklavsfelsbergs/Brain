# Carrier tender / procurement consulting market

As of: 2026-06-09

- Market splits into four tiers: US parcel-rate-negotiation boutiques (gainshare), freight+parcel spend-management mid-firms, big-firm strategic sourcing (McKinsey/Kearney/BCG/EY, day-rate, enterprise-only), and EU freight-tender consultancies/platforms (Staedtler, Caestra, Miebach, 4flow, Transporeon, Freightos).
- Gainshare / no-savings-no-fee dominates the US boutique tier: TransImpact, Shipware, Renaissance Advisory, AFMS, Parcel Consulting LLC. Near-zero engagement risk is the core sales lever; actual gainshare percentages are not published.
- Vendor savings claims cluster 15-40% range, ~21-24% average (Shipware 21.5%, TransImpact 23.6%) - all self-reported, no independent audit located.
- The rigorous methodology bar: re-rate 12 months of shipment-level history against actual rate cards. Most firms instead sell proprietary benchmark-database comparison, which is coarser and rarely transparent.
- Big firms do not gainshare and do not re-rate at shipment grain; parcel is a sub-line of strategic sourcing, enterprise only.
- EU side is freight-tender platforms and network consulting, NOT parcel-rate boutiques. No EU equivalent of the US gainshare parcel boutique surfaced - but this is an absence-of-evidence finding from English-language search, not proven absence; a German/Dutch/French sweep is the named follow-up.
- White space (strongest signal): European mid-market multi-carrier parcel re-rating (DHL/DPD/Hermes/GLS/UPS mixes) at shipment-level rigor, fast AND methodologically transparent (shown math vs TransImpact's black-box 48h claim).
- Mid-market EU e-commerce shippers sit between the tiers: too small for big firms, outside the US boutiques' carrier coverage.
