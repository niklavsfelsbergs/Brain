# Ģertrūdes iela 113 — price fairness & the domājamās-daļas resale reality

**Subject.** A central-Riga (Centrs) apartment at **Ģertrūdes iela 113**: fully-renovated pre-war brick ("P. kara", *mūra*), 2 isolated rooms, **58 m²**, floor **5/6**, asking **€120,000 (€2,068.97/m²)**, land owned, individual gas heating. Held — per the brief — as **domājamās daļas** (ideal co-ownership share of the whole building, *kopīpašums*), **not** a separately-titled *dzīvokļa īpašums*. Listing: https://www.ss.lv/msg/lv/real-estate/flats/riga/centre/befhkm.html

**Two questions.** (A) Is €2,069/m² fair for renovated pre-war Centrs stock in 2026? (B) What is the realistic resale discount for an ideal-share apartment, who buys them, and how hard is the exit?

**Date.** 2026-05-25. **Author.** Penguin (research operative), Zezima namespace.

**Confidence headline.** *Price-fairness (A): high* on the comp anchors, which converge cleanly. *Share-discount magnitude (B): low-to-medium* — I found **one clean, directly-comparable share listing** (renovated pre-war Centrs, explicitly marked *domājamā daļa no kopīpašuma*) plus corroborating market data, which lets me give a **defensible discount range** for the first time — but it rests on a thin sample of asking prices, not achieved transactions. Read the range as directional, not precise.

---

## A note the listing itself raises

The ss.lv listing does **not** state the ownership structure. The fetched listing text covers price, area, renovation, heating, parking, and "Zeme īpašumā" (land owned) — but says nothing about *domājamā daļa* / *kopīpašums* / *dzīvokļa īpašums* (https://www.ss.lv/msg/lv/real-estate/flats/riga/centre/befhkm.html). The share-ownership fact comes from the brief, presumably from the Zemesgrāmata or a viewing, not the ad. **Confirm it in the Land Register before anything else** — and note that a listing *omitting* the structure is itself mildly informative: sellers of clean *dzīvokļa īpašums* tend to say so; silence often hides a share. *(inference)*

## Part A — Is €2,069/m² fair?

### A1. The right comp set: renovated pre-war, Centrs. Two independent 2026 reports converge near €3,000/m². **(high)**

- **investoruklubs.lv, 2026-05-19** (an analyst column on Riga pricing, April 2026 data) puts **renovated pre-war Centre at "3000 EUR" per m²**, new projects in the Centre at "3800 EUR/m²", and secondary-market Centre at "2900 EUR/m²" — all up "teju 10%" (nearly 10%) year-over-year. https://www.investoruklubs.lv/birzas-jaunumi/2026/05/19/par-rukisu-dzivokli-centra-prasa-vairak-neka-par-jauno-projektu-apkaimes
- **Latio market report (2025)** — at July 2025, **renovated buildings in the Centre = "2 500 EUR/m²"**, new projects Centre "3 400 EUR/m²", secondary new-build Centre "2 960 EUR/m²". https://latio.lv/lv/Majoklu_tirgus_atdzivojas

The two are consistent: Latio's €2,500 (mid-2025) growing ~10% YoY lands right around investoruklubs' €3,000 (spring 2026). So the **renovated-pre-war-Centrs asking benchmark in 2026 is ~€2,900–3,100/m²**.

### A2. Against that benchmark, €2,069/m² is ~30–33% BELOW a clean-title equivalent. **(high, as an asking-vs-asking comparison)**

€2,069 / €3,000 = 0.69 → roughly a **31% discount** to the renovated-pre-war-Centre asking benchmark. For a *fully-renovated* flat (restored fireplaces, individual gas boiler, custom kitchen, a building that got a new roof and a 6th-floor addition), that is conspicuously cheap. **The ownership structure is the most likely explanation** — the price already looks like a share-discounted price, not a clean-title price (this is the bridge to Part B).

### A3. But "asking benchmark" overstates achieved prices — use VZD for ground truth. **(high)**

Portal headline €/m² figures are *asking* and skew to nicer stock. The authoritative *achieved* (median transaction) source is the **State Land Service (VZD)**, last updated 2026-05-22:

- Series-built apartments (pre-2000), highest in **Rīga centre ~1800 EUR/m²** (2025);
- New developments, Rīga centre **~2900 EUR/m²** (2025);
- These are **median** prices for residential-space-group transactions. https://www.vzd.gov.lv/lv/videjas-darijumu-cenas-dzivokliem

VZD doesn't isolate "renovated pre-war" as a class, but the takeaway holds: **achieved Centre prices run materially below portal asking**, and a renovated pre-war flat sits between the series-built median (~€1,800) and the new-build median (~€2,900). A fair *achieved* price for this flat as a clean title is plausibly **~€2,400–2,900/m²** — so even against achieved (not asking) prices, €2,069/m² is at or below the bottom of the clean-title band.

### A4. Market trend: rising, not flat — Latvia is in an up-leg, less frothy than peers. **(high)**

- 2025 was a clear recovery: transaction count "būtiski palielinās", buyer confidence strengthening, prices "pakāpeniski sāk pieaugt" (Latio, https://latio.lv/lv/Majoklu_tirgus_atdzivojas). Time-to-sell dropped to **~55 days**, 16% of homes sold within a month of listing (same source).
- **2026 forecast: existing apartments ~7–10% appreciation**, existing dwellings outpacing new construction (~9.4% vs ~3.8% annual). Centre + Vecrīga are the priciest, €2,900–7,000/m² range cited; reports cross-reference ARCO and Latio. https://investropa.com/blogs/news/riga-housing-prices , https://investropa.com/blogs/news/latvia-price-forecasts
- This matches the brief's prior: Latvia lags / is less frothy than the broader Baltic, but it is **in an up-cycle in 2026**, not flat. (Treat the third-party aggregator forecasts as *medium* confidence — they synthesize broker data rather than report it primary.)

### A5. The €100–160k 2-room band liquidity. **(medium)**

This is the most liquid slice of the Centre market — small renovated flats move fastest (micro-apartments 15–30 m² cited at €3,160/m², +13% YoY, selling at €70–90k; investoruklubs.lv). A 58 m² 2-room renovated flat at €120k is squarely in the band where demand is deepest **for clean titles**. Comparable *clean-title* asking examples seen in passing: a 62 m² renovated 2-room at €199,000 and a 58 m² renovated 2-room (1st floor) at €94,940 (MM.lv search surfacing, https://mm.lv/dzivokli-riga-centrs ) — a wide spread driven by floor, exact street, and finish. The €94,940 one (58 m² ≈ €1,637/m², ground floor) shows the bottom of the clean-title 2-room range overlaps the share-discounted range, so floor and street matter as much as title at the margin.

**Part A verdict.** As a *clean-title* renovated pre-war 2-room in Centrs, **€2,069/m² would be a good-to-cheap price** — ~30% under the asking benchmark, at/under the bottom of the achieved-price band, in a rising market. That very cheapness is the tell: **the price is consistent with a share-discounted flat, not a bargain clean title.** The fairness question collapses into Part B — is the discount big enough to pay for the share risk?

## Part B — The domājamās-daļas resale reality

Prior research (`2026-05-23-domajamas-dalas-resale-and-conversion.md`) established the discount's *direction* (unanimous) but could not pin *magnitude* — the only number was an unreliable ~90% proportional-valuation anecdote. This pass adds **actual market comparables**.

### B1. THE comp: a renovated pre-war Centrs flat, explicitly a share, asking ~€1,860/m². **(medium — single comp, asking price)**

**Bruņinieku iela 28, Rīga Centrs.** A 1910 Eižens Laube building, façade renovated 2019, autonomous gas heat, fireplace, sauna, heated bathroom floors. **129 m², 4 rooms, 5th floor, €240,000 → €1,860/m².** The listing states verbatim: **"Īpašums ir reģistrēts kā domājamā daļa no kopīpašuma"** ("the property is registered as an ideal share of co-ownership"). https://www.city24.lv/real-estate/apartments-for-sale/riga-centrs-bruninieku-iela/7049052

This is the cleanest possible comparator: renovated, pre-war, Centrs, *explicitly a share* — exactly the subject's profile.

- vs the **renovated-pre-war-Centre asking benchmark (~€3,000/m²)**: €1,860 / €3,000 = 0.62 → a **~38% discount**.
- The subject at **€2,069/m²** sits *above* this share comp (€1,860) but *below* the clean-title benchmark — consistent with "a share, but a smaller/nicer/more-liquid one in a building that's clearly been invested in."

**Caveats, stated honestly:** (1) it's one listing; (2) it's an *asking* price, and shares may need to cut more than clean titles to actually close, so the *achieved* discount is plausibly deeper than 38%; (3) 129 m²/4-room is a thinner, slower market than 58 m²/2-room regardless of title, which drags its €/m² down independently of the share issue. So **~38% is a floor-ish read on the asking discount, not a precise transaction discount.**

### B2. Triangulated discount range. **(low-to-medium)**

Combining the one hard comp (~38% asking discount) with the subject's own implied position (~30% below benchmark) and the qualitative consensus from prior research (banks won't lend → cash-only → smaller pool):

> **Best estimate: a realistic resale discount of ~25–45% vs an equivalent clean-title (*dzīvokļa īpašums*) flat**, centred around **a third off**. The wide band reflects that the discount is driven mostly by the *financing/buyer-pool* penalty, which varies with how clean the rest of the file is (registered *lietošanas kārtība*? few co-owners? *pusmāja* vs carved-up block?). A clean *pusmāja* share with a registered use-arrangement and Altum-eligibility sits at the shallow end; a messy multi-apartment share with no registered use-arrangement sits at the deep end or worse.

This **supersedes the ≈90% anecdote** as the working number. 90% was a proportional-valuation artifact (valuing a fractional slice of the whole building in the abstract), never a market price for a usable apartment. The market discount for a *usable, occupied, renovated* apartment-as-share is far shallower — meaningful tens-of-percent, not near-total.

### B3. Who buys them — cash buyers dominate; financing has NOT meaningfully widened the pool. **(medium)**

- Every Latvian source agrees banks "skatās ar aizdomām uz domājamo daļu iegādi" (view share purchases with suspicion) and the market value "visticamāk, būs zemāka par analoga dzīvokļa īpašuma vērtību" (LV portāls, via search; https://lvportals.lv/skaidrojumi/328256-kas-jazina-iegadajoties-dalas-kopipasuma-2021 ).
- The one structural exception from prior research stands: **Altum explicitly supports buying a *domājamā daļa* of a *pusmāja* (half-house)** via guaranteed loans through partner banks — so a *narrow* slice of share purchases is financeable. That does NOT extend to ideal shares of a **multi-apartment block**, which is the harder case and the one most likely to apply at a 6-flat-plus pre-war building like Ģertrūdes 113. *(inference — building's co-owner count unconfirmed.)*
- Net: **the buyer pool is still predominantly cash**, which is the real mechanism behind the discount. I found **no 2025–2026 evidence** that bank/Altum financing of *multi-apartment* shares has widened. **(null result — flagged as a gap, not a "no.")**

### B4. Time-on-market for shares — I could NOT pin a number. **(null result)**

The general Centre market sells in ~55 days median (Latio). I found **no source quantifying how much longer share listings sit.** The Bruņinieku comp's days-on-market wasn't retrievable. Directionally, a cash-only buyer pool + the exit-friction features below imply a materially longer marketing period than clean titles, but I have no figure. *(gap)*

### B5. Exit friction — real but bounded; the bigger deterrent is financing, not the pre-emption clock. **(medium)**

From prior research (well-anchored there), the two statutory frictions:
- **Pirmpirkuma tiesības (first refusal), 2 months** — co-owners must be offered the share on the buyer's terms; 2-month clock from written notice. https://pardotipasumu.lv/2023/12/29/ka-pardot-ipasuma-domajamas-dalas/
- **Izpirkuma tiesība (redemption), 1 year** — a co-owner can reclaim the share within a year of the outsider's registration in the Land Register (same source). Keeps the title "unclean" for a year post-sale.

**How much do these actually deter buyers in practice?** My read *(inference, consistent with the consensus sources)*: the 2-month first-refusal is a *delay and uncertainty* tax more than a deal-killer — it rarely triggers (co-owners usually don't have cash to match), but it adds ~2 months and the risk the deal evaporates. The 1-year redemption overhang is the scarier one *on paper* (a buyer's title isn't fully secure for a year) but is also rarely exercised. **The dominant, everyday deterrent is the financing wall (B3)** — most buyers simply can't get a mortgage, which removes them before the pre-emption clock ever matters. The pre-emption/redemption features prune the *cash* buyers who'd otherwise be willing; the mortgage problem prunes *everyone else*. The mortgage problem is the bigger cut.

### B6. The escape hatch still stands (from prior research). **(high, mechanics)**

Conversion to *dzīvokļa īpašums* via the VZD division process erases the discount — but needs **unanimous consent of all co-owners** (one holdout halts it; court-ordered forced dissolution is the only fallback). VZD fees are small (~€40 opinion + ~€30–45/apartment); the **cadastral survey** if not yet done is the variable cost. If conversion is realistic *here* (few, amenable co-owners), the €120k entry could later become a normal liquid flat — that optionality is part of what the discount is paying for. See `2026-05-23-domajamas-dalas-resale-and-conversion.md` §B.

**Part B verdict.** The discount is real and now has a number behind it: **~a third off (range ~25–45%) vs clean title**, driven mainly by the cash-only buyer pool, deeper for messy/multi-apartment shares and shallower for clean *pusmāja*-with-registered-use-arrangement cases. The subject's €2,069/m² is *inside* the discounted band but at its shallow end (above the one hard share comp). Whether that's *fair* depends entirely on the file specifics from prior research's checklist — is there a registered *lietošanas kārtība*, how many co-owners, is conversion feasible — which only a Zemesgrāmata pull and a viewing answer.

## Synthesis: is the price fair?

- **As a clean title:** €2,069/m² is cheap (~30% under benchmark, in a rising market). If this were a normal *dzīvokļa īpašums*, it'd be a clear buy.
- **As a share (which it is):** the price is *consistent with* a ~30% share discount — i.e. the seller has **already priced in roughly the discount the market demands**, putting it at the *shallow* end of the ~25–45% range. That means the buyer is **not** getting an extra bargain for taking on share risk; they're paying close to fair value *for a share*. The upside case rests on (a) conversion being feasible (which would re-rate it to ~€3,000/m² clean-title territory — a ~45% paper gain) and (b) a registered use-arrangement securing the specific flat. The downside case: if conversion is blocked and no use-arrangement is registered, the discount may not be deep enough to compensate, and resale will be slow and cash-only.

**One-line:** the price is *fair-for-a-share, not a steal* — the discount is already in it; the whole question is whether the file specifics (use-arrangement, co-owner count, conversion feasibility) justify accepting a share at the shallow end of the discount band.

## Gaps & open questions

- **Share-discount magnitude still rests on ONE hard comp** (Bruņinieku 28, ~38% asking discount) plus the subject's own implied ~30%. A robust number needs 5–10 share listings vs matched clean titles, ideally *achieved* prices — which ss.lv/city24 list pages don't expose in fetchable form (the ownership phrase lives in the body text, not the summary). The ~25–45% range is my best honest estimate, not a measured figure. **(biggest gap)**
- **Achieved vs asking.** Every share number I have is an *asking* price; shares likely cut more to close, so the true achieved discount may be deeper. VZD medians don't tag ownership structure, so I can't isolate share transactions.
- **Time-on-market for shares: unquantified** (B4). No source put a number on how much longer shares sit.
- **This building's co-owner count / structure.** Pusmāja vs carved-up multi-apartment block changes both the discount and the conversion feasibility — and the ss.lv listing doesn't say. Unconfirmed.
- **Is a *lietošanas kārtība* registered for this flat?** The hinge from prior research; can't be answered from listings — needs the Zemesgrāmata.
- **Financing-of-shares 2025–2026:** no evidence the pool has widened for multi-apartment shares (B3) — a null result; a call to Altum + a partner-bank desk would close it.
- **LSM "bumba ar laika degli" article** (https://www.lsm.lv/raksts/dzive--stils/ikdienai/specialisti-ipasumu-domajamo-dalu-iegade--bumba-ar-laika-degli.a470886/ ) and **santa.lv adv. Radziņš piece** remain blocked/paywalled — likely the richest qualitative reads if Niklavs opens them directly.
